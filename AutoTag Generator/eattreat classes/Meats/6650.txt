6650	Tried The Prawn Toast At Social? Here, Try This Super Simple Recipe	<p>Crispy crunchy prawn on toast that is exactly what the doctor ordered. Seafood-y goodness, we usually like our prawns whole, but the comfort of eating your staple with a side like this is an opportunity that we cannot pass up. Try this easy prawn toast recipe when you want to jazz things up.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2-3</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 5 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 5 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">250 gm prawns, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 egg white</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 clove garlic</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 teaspoons Szechuan sauce</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon corn flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 spring onions</p><p style="text-align: center;">A pinch of ginger (optional)</p><p style="text-align: center;">8 slices white bread, crusts removed, each slice cut into 4 triangles</p><p style="text-align: center;">Sesame seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">Oil</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Method</strong></h4><p>Place the chopped prawns, egg white, garlic, Szechuan sauce, corn flour, salt, spring onions and ginger in a blender and grind until you obtain a paste. Spread the paste onto a triangle of bread before pressing face down onto a plate of sesame seeds (you want to get as many seeds to stick as possible).</p><p>Heat oil in a pan, add the bread triangles and fry for 30-50 seconds on each side. Serve hot and crispy with a generous dollop of hot and sour sauce.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
