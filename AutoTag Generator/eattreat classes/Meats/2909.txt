2909	These Smoked Bacon Pancakes Are Savoury Pancakes Done Right!	<p>Pancakes with a savoury twist of bacon strips glazed in maple syrup!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 15 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 15 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">250 grams bacon</p><p style="text-align: center;">Maple syrup</p><p style="text-align: center;">150 grams plain flour</p><p style="text-align: center;"> 1 teaspoon baking powder</p><p style="text-align: center;"> A pinch of salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">120 ml milk</p><p style="text-align: center;"> 3 eggs</p><p style="text-align: center;"> Vegetable oil</p><p style="text-align: center;"> 1½ tablespoon butter</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Heat oil in a frying pan, add the strips of bacon and fry until each side is golden and crisp. This should take about 2-3 minutes. Once you remove the strips from the pan, drain the excess fat.</p><p>Add a tablespoon of maple syrup and wait for it to start bubbling. Put the strips back into the pan and cook for about 2 minutes until the strips seem perfectly glazed. Set these aside and keep warm.</p><p>To prepare your pancake batter, sieve the flour, baking powder and salt into a bowl and make a well in the centre.</p><p>In a separate bowl whisk together the milk and the eggs. Carefully pour this mixture into the well and gradually stir until your batter is smooth and thick.</p><p>In a frying pan, warm a little oil. Now, add a spoonful of the batter mixture and shallow fry for about 30-60 seconds until each side is golden and puffed.  Transfer the cooked pancakes to a warm plate and cover with foil.</p><p>Stack your pancakes and place the glazed bacon strips on the top. You can top it off with a dollop of butter and drizzle it with extra maple syrup.</p><p><strong>#EatExtra:</strong> You can add tiny bacon bits in the pancake batter too!</p><p><em><strong>Photo courtesy of:</strong> Madishetty Manasa</em></p>
