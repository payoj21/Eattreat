8537	Deep Fried Fish Pakora In A Spicy Batter For Your Tea Time Munchies	<p>Bhetki or basa left in freezer? Have guests over and don&#8217;t know what to plate up? Here&#8217;s an idea:<a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/fish-and-chips-recipe/"> Fish finger </a>desi-style. Go the fun way with this fish pakora recipe instead of your boring besan or onion bhajjis. Spicy, fulfilling and tasty, these fish pakoras will be the star of your kitchen, after you, of course!</p><p><strong>Serves: </strong>3-4&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Prep Time: </strong>10 minutes, 30 minutes for marinade</p><p><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 15 minutes&nbsp;</p><h4><strong>Ingredients:</strong></h4><p>Oil</p><p><i>For the marinade</i></p><p>½ lemon, juiced</p><p>½ teaspoon salt</p><p>1½ teaspoons grated ginger</p><p>2 cloves garlic, finely chopped</p><p>1 teaspoon garam masala</p><p>1 teaspoon chaat masala</p><p>1 chilli, finely chopped</p><p>400 grams fish, cut into large chunks</p><p><i>For the batter</i></p><p>100 grams gram flour (besan)</p><p>½ teaspoon salt</p><p>A handful of coriander leaves, chopped</p><p>1 teaspoon chilli powder</p><p>1 teaspoon baking powder</p><p>Water</p><h4><strong>Method:</strong></h4><p><strong>Marinade</strong></p><p>In a bowl, mix together the lemon juice, salt, ginger, garlic, garam masala, chaat masala and chopped chilli until you get a uniform, dry paste. Generously coat each of the fish chunks in this marinade mixture, and set aside in the refrigerator for a minimum of 30 minutes (the longer the better).</p><p><strong>Batter</strong></p><p>Sieve the gram flour into a bowl, and add in the salt, coriander leaves, chilli powder, baking powder and a little bit of water. Beat the mixture until you achieve a thick batter.</p><p>Remove the marinated chunks of fish from the refrigerator and dip them in the batter, making sure that all sides are evenly coated.&nbsp;</p><p>Heat oil in a pan on low-medium heat and gently fry the fish for about three minutes, until crisp and golden brown. Remove with a slotted spoon, drain on a kitchen towel, and serve hot with lemon wedges.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of:&nbsp;<strong>Azra Sadr</strong></em></p>
