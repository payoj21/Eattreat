6767	Nisteachi Coddi Recipe For A Goan Fish Curry To Remember For Ever	<p>Spicy fish curry in true Goan style! A Goan fish curry recipe to boot, we obviously cook this when our Goa plans fall through, which is usually all the time. The only silver lining seems to be the opening of <a href="http://eattreat.in/goan-cuisine-in-delhi-lady-baga/">Lady Baga in Delhi </a>and this Goan fish curry recipe.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><b>Serves: </b>2-4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><b>Prep Time: </b>10-15 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><b>Cook Time: </b>20-25 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><b>Ingredients:</b></p><p style="text-align: center;">5 whole dry red Kashmiri chillis</p><p style="text-align: center;">¾ cup grated coconut</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon cumin seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">Water</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup vegetable oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 large onion, grated</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 sprig curry leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 medium tomatoes, peeled and chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">4 green chillis, slit</p><p style="text-align: center;">15 grams tamarind, soaked in ½ cup hot water for 15 minutes, pulp extracted</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 medium pomfrets, cut into 2½ cm filets</p><p style="text-align: center;"><b>Method:</b></p><p>Grind together the red chillis, grated coconut and cumin seeds. Add in a little water and continue to grind until a paste forms. Set aside.</p><p>Heat oil in a wok and fry the onions until golden brown. Add the paste along with the turmeric powder, and cook for about five minutes, stirring constantly. Next add the curry leaves, tomatoes and ¼ cup water. Stir occasionally and cook till the tomatoes are pulpy. Add in the green chillis, tamarind pulp, salt and one cup of water and bring to a boil.</p><p>Add the fish and stir gently. Cover with a lid and cook for another 10 minutes on low-medium heat. Serve piping hot with a side of steamed rice.</p><p><em>Representative Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p>
