5227	Nigiri Or Maki Sushi: A Dummy’s Guide To Eating Sushi	<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Having sushi for the first time can be an intimidating experience if you don’t know much about it. Mostly regarded as an acquired taste, sushi is now fast catching on in India with <a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//asian-haus-pan-asian-chinese-delivery/">many restaurants </a>now offering nigiri or maki sushi on their menus. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’re new to the sushi bandwagon, we’ve made a list of things you ought to know to help you get started.</span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>#1: Know Thy Basics</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sushi is all about the rice. A special short-grain type of rice is seasoned with a mix of rice vinegar, salt and sugar – this mixture, known as </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">shari </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">is sort of sticky, and can be shaped to form the N</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">igiri </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">or rolled to form the</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Maki. They also hve <a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//instagram-trend-sushi-burrito-japanese-fusion/">sushi burritos </a>now, just saying.</span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>#2: Get The Names Right</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For the beginners, getting the background right could be useful. For instance, the </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Maki</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (meaning “roll” in Japanese), is the sushi roll. The four most common </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Maki</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> rolls are</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">: <strong>Hosomaki</strong>, <strong>Futomaki</strong></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><strong>Uramaki </strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">and the </span><strong>Temaki</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Nigiri</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is a hand-formed sushi using a mound of rice pressed into a rectangle by the chef’s palms and topped with </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">neta</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (common toppings include salmon, tuna and shrimp). A cross between the </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">nigiri</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and the </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">maki sushi</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">, the </span><strong>gunamaki</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is a clump of rice wrapped with a wide band of </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">nori</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (dried seaweed).</span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>#3: Eat It Right</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While it is assumed that sushi is best eaten with chopsticks, there is actually no hard and fast rule. In fact, it’s actually better to eat </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">maki</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> rolls and </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">nigiri</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> with your fingers. Also, don’t use wasabi with all types of sushi – </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Nigiri</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> in particular. Wasabi is to be eaten carefully, as it is very strong, pungent and flavourful. Dab your sushi with wasabi, and never mix wasabi with soy sauce (</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">shoyu</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">). Pickled ginger (</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">gari</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">) is best used as a palate cleanser in between bites, and not as a topping.</span></p><p><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/12/sushi-932686_1920.jpg"><img title = "Nigiri Or Maki Sushi: A Dummy&#039;s Guide To Eating Sushi  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5263" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/12/sushi-932686_1920.jpg" alt = "seafood restaurants japanese Category:Restaurants asian   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/sushi-932686_1920.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/sushi-932686_1920-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/sushi-932686_1920-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>#4: Eat At A Recommended Restaurant With Friends</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When trying sushi for the first time, it’s best to go for a restaurant that’s known or recommended by a friend. Go with friends, preferably those who are not new to <a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//love-japanese-order-guppy-ai/">Japanese cuisine</a>, if only for the comfort of having buddies with you to tell you more about this exciting fare.</span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>#5: Don’t Stress, Just Dig In!</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">After these basics, we’re sure you feel a whole lot more comfortable with the idea of eating sushi. After all, all the world’s a plate just waiting to be tasted. So keep in mind that you may not like it at first, but for some of you – it might just be love at first (measured) bite!</span></p><p><em>Photos Courtesy Of: <strong>Pixabay</strong></em></p>
