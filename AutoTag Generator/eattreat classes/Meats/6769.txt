6769	Prawn Sambal Recipe: A Malaysian Import That You Won’t Get Enough Of	<p>A Malaysian import to recreate at home that we&#8217;d love to partake in every meal we get a chance. This Prawn Sambal (Sambal Udang) recipe takes a total of 20 minutes to prep and cook and if that doesn&#8217;t give you a reason to get cooking, we don&#8217;t know what will. This creamy gravy is to die for in this seafood recipe, so make sure you get that right.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves: </strong>4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time: </strong>10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time: </strong>10 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2½ medium onions, roughly chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">5 cloves garlic</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 lemongrass stalks (only the white parts), roughly chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">4 red chillis</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons vegetable oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon belcehan (fermented shrimp paste)</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon ground turmeric</p><p style="text-align: center;">500 grams prawns, peeled</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/3 cup water, boiled</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons tamarind concentrate</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 teaspoons light soy sauce</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Grind the onions, garlic, lemongrass and chillis in a blender and set aside.</p><p>Heat oil in a pan, and add in the spice mixture and belcehan paste. Fry while stirring continuously. Stir in the turmeric before adding in the prawns along with the boiled water. Give everything a good mix.</p><p>Add the tamarind, sugar, salt and soy sauce and continue to stir until the prawns are pink and cooked through (prawns cook really fast so be careful not to overdo them). Serve this Prawn Sambal (Sambal Udang) recipe with steamed rice and a side of fried veggies.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/avlxyz/4143662296">Alpha via Flickr</a></strong></em></p>
