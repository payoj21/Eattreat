6559	This Recipe For Baked Dijon Glazed Salmon With Herb Crust Will Rescue Work Nights	<p>Salmon baked with a twist. We love ourselves some well-done fish and when it comes with a Dijon glaze, it&#8217;s easy to call this situation heaven. So try this easy baked salmon recipe that&#8217;ll be done in 45 minutes and we can tell you, it&#8217;s easy on the eyes and perfect for the palate.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 20 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 15 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><b></b><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup butter, melted</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 tablespoons Dijon mustard</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons honey</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup dry breadcrumbs</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup finely chopped pistachios</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 salmon fillets</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt and pepper to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 lemon, for garnish</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Method</strong><b></b></h4><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Place the butter, Dijon mustard and honey in a bowl and stir well. Set aside.</p><p>In another bowl, mix together the breadcrumbs, pistachios and parsley. Set aside.</p><p>Lightly brush the salmon fillets with the honey mustard mixture, coating each side evenly. Sprinkle the top layers of the fillets with the bread crumb mixture.</p><p>Place the coated salmon fillets on a baking tray and set in a preheated oven (200 degrees Celsius) to bake for about 12-15 minutes till the salmon is cooked through.</p><p>Season the fillets with salt and pepper, garnish with a wedge of lemon and serve hot.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Pixabay</strong></em></p>
