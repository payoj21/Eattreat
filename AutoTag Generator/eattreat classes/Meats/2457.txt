A Mutton Keema Recipe From Social Offline’s Kitchens	<p>There are times when we honestly just want to hitch a ride to Bombay, but more often than not, we’re disappointed with ourselves. But anywho, since we couldn&#8217;t make it to The Bay this weekend we decided to get some Bambaiyaa flavour into our kitchens. So here’s presenting Social Offline’s gorgeous mutton keema recipe.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 20 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">150 grams mutton keema</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 onion, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">2-3 garlic cloves, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tomato, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon ginger, grated</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon red chilli powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon dhaniya powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon cumin power</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon MDH kitchen king masala</p><p style="text-align: center;">50 grams green peas</p><p style="text-align: center;">30 grams fresh coriander, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 Egg</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p style="text-align: left;">Preheat your oven to 150 degree Celsius.</p><p style="text-align: left;">In a pan, sauté the onion, garlic, ginger and tomato for 2-3 minutes, until golden brown.&nbsp;Add in all the dry spices.</p><p style="text-align: left;">Meanwhile, add the peas into simmer water and boil for a while. Once they’re cooked, throw them along with the keema into the cooked masala and mix well. Toss this for about five to seven minutes until the keema is cooked.</p><p style="text-align: left;">Mix in the fresh coriander, break an egg over it all and bake in the oven for two to three minutes. Garnish with coriander and serve with <em>bun maska</em>!</p><p style="text-align: left;"><em>Photos Courtesy Of: <strong>Azra Sadr</strong></em></p><p style="text-align: left;">By Social Offline</p>
