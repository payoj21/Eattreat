7023	Tom Kha Gai Is The Healing Thai Chicken Soup For Runny Noses & Sore Throats	<p>Rich, creamy and aromatic, this Tom Kha Gai recipe is our favourite rendition of a quintessential<a href="http://eattreat.in/organic-red-thai-curry-devang-house-kitchen/"> Thai soup meal</a>. Packed with<a href="http://eattreat.in/andreas-eatery-food-around-the-world/"> authentic Thai herbs</a> (lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, bird’s eye chilli and the fragrant galangal) and cooked in the creamy goodness of coconut milk, this classic and flavourful broth is our go-to Thai soup!</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 stocks fresh lemongrass (outer layers removed)</p><p style="text-align: center;">1-inch piece of galangal/ginger</p><p style="text-align: center;">10 kafir lime leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">6 cups chicken broth</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 whole chicken, shredded</p><p style="text-align: center;">250 grams shiitake mushrooms</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 can coconut milk</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons fish sauce</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon brown sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">Chilli oil, cilantro leaves, and lime wedges – for garnish</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 garlic cloves, crushed</p><p style="text-align: center;">White pepper to season</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 limes, zested &amp; juiced</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Heat the chicken broth in a saucepan.</p><p>Add the smashed lemongrass, galangal and kafir lime leaves and let it cook on simmer for eight to 10 minutes to let the flavors seep into the broth.</p><p>In the meanwhile, soak the shitake in a bowl of warm water and let them soften.</p><p>Add the shredded chicken to the simmering broth; Bring to a boil and let cook for another 20 to 25 minutes (until chicken softens).</p><p>Stir in the coconut milk, fish sauce, sugar and the lemon zest and juice.</p>
