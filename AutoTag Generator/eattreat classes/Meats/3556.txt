3556	A Rich, Spicy Mutton Masala For Your Weekend Lunch	<p>We aren&#8217;t big supporters of rich gravies and spicy meat, but Summer is almost gone and we can feel the temperatures going lower. So, it isn&#8217;t really a bad thing to crave some Mutton Masala on a day like this. A big lunch of this and paratha, and a siesta for a good day.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves: </strong>3</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time: </strong>30 mins</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons ghee</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 &#8211; 4 bay leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 &#8211; 4 cloves</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 black cardamom pod</p><p style="text-align: center;">1-inch cinnamon stick</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon cumin</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 onions, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste</p><p style="text-align: center;">250 grams mutton</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon water</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon coriander powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon garam masala</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon chilli powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon mutton masala</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tomatoes, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 spring onion, finely chopped for garnish</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p><b> </b>Heat the ghee in a saucepan, add the bay leaves, cloves, black cardamom, cinnamon and cumin. Add the salt and the onions and cook for two minutes. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another five minutes.</p><p>Now add in the mutton. Dissolve the coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, chilli powder and mutton masala in a tablespoon of water and pour over the mutton. Cook for about 10 minutes before adding the tomatoes. Cook further for about five minutes.</p><p>Transfer to a serving plate, sprinkle with some chopped spring onion and serve!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
