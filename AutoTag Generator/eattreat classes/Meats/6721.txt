6721	Maple Salmon: A Fish Dish That’s Just Wayy Too Dishy For A Regular Meal	<p>A simple and sweet salmon dish that just has a hint of Canadian maple syrup. If you&#8217;re looking for salmon recipes that out of the ordinary, we&#8217;d definitely recommend this. We love ourselves some rare pink salmon and if it comes with a maple glaze, then there&#8217;s probably nothing like it for a hearty dinner.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes, 30 minutes resting time</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 15 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 salmon fillets (roughly 200 gm each)</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup maple syrup</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 tablespoons light soy sauce</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cloves garlic, minced</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon black pepper</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula</strong></h4><p>Whisk together the maple syrup, soy sauce and minced garlic in a bowl. Place the salmon fillets on a tray and pour the maple syrup mixture over them (make sure you pour over both sides). Cover and place in the refrigerator for 30 minutes.</p><p>Place the excess marinade mixture in a saucepan and bring to a boil while continuously stirring. Set aside to use as a glaze.</p><p>Bake the coated salmon fillets in a preheated oven (250 degrees Celsius) for about 15 minutes till the salmon is cooked through. Drizzle over the maple glaze, garnish with black pepper and serve hot.</p><p>Salmon recipes are dime a dozen, but if find some stellar<a href="http://eattreat.in/tag/seafood/"> fish and seafood recipes here</a>.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
