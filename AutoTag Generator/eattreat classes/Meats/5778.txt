5778	7 State Bhavans To Get Your Fix Of Authentic Indian Food	<p>Craving an authentic ethnic meal, but don’t have too much dough to spend? Want to do a Bharat-darshan, but don’t want to leave the city? We’ve got you covered with our list of favourite State Bhavans with their Indian food. With the many state bhavans in Delhi offering a crazy variety of authentic, delicious fare, you can feast on ethnic delights without spending a bomb.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Andhra Bhavan</strong></h4><p>One of our favourite breakfast joints in the city, Andhra Bhavan packs a south Indian punch with its breakfast thali. We also love visiting Andhra Bhavan at dinner time, with our pick being the vegetarian <strong>thali</strong> (Andhra style sabzi, dal, roti, curd and dessert) with a side order of <strong>chicken fry</strong>, and <strong>prawn curry</strong>. Their <strong>fish curry</strong> is super tangy, and also worth a try.</p><p>Drop in at 1, Ashoka Road, Feroze Shah Road</p><p><strong>Cost for two:</strong> INR 300</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Gujarat Bhavan</strong></h4><p>We recommend visiting Gujarat Bhavan when you’re starving, because the sheer quantity of food will leave you satiated. Expect a homely Gujarati meal when you order their thali, which has <strong>Light Gujarati dal</strong>, <strong>Bhakadwadi, fresh theplas or rotis, sabzi, Farsaan, Kathod with pickle and papad</strong>, accompanied by a glass of fresh buttermilk. Gluttony guaranteed.</p><p>Drop in at 11, Kautilya Marg, Chanakyapuri</p><p><strong>Cost for two:</strong> INR 350</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Banga Bhavan</strong></h4><p>Run and operated by Bijoli Grill, Banga Bhavan does authentic Bengali food cooked the traditional way. Our picks would be the <strong>Kosha Mangsho</strong> (spicy Bengali mutton curry) with luchi. You could also try their fish – dig into <strong>Sorse Hilsa</strong>, <strong>Bhapa Hilsa</strong>,<strong> Fish Paturi</strong> with steamed rice. Also don’t miss their <strong>Mishti Doi</strong>.</p><p>Drop in at 3, Hailey Rd, Connaught Place, Vakil Lane, Mandi House</p><p><strong>Cost for two:</strong> INR 650</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Karnataka Sangha</strong></h4><p>Head here for all your South Indian food cravings. You might get a waiting line there, but that’s just testament to the amazing food that Karnataka Sangha dishes out. Their piping hot Sambar is simply the best. A must try here would be the<strong> Mysore Masala Dosa</strong>, crisp with a spicy layer of chilli paste.</p><p><strong>Cost for two:</strong> INR 400</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Goa Niwas</strong></h4><p><a href="http://eattreat.in/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/Goan_Fish_Curry.jpg"><img title = "7 State Bhavans To Get Your Fix Of Authentic Indian Food  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5781" src="http://eattreat.in/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/Goan_Fish_Curry.jpg" alt = "north Indian goan fish dosa Chanakyapuri New Delhi Category:Meats bengali andhra   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/Goan_Fish_Curry.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/Goan_Fish_Curry-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/Goan_Fish_Curry-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p><p>Prawn craving? Head to Goa Niwas, for simple, authentic and homely Goan food. Try the <strong>Goan fish curry</strong>, with steamed rice and order <strong>Butter Garlic Prawns</strong>, or <strong>Prawn Peri Peri</strong> (Yum!). Also don’t miss their chocolate pudding.</p><p>Drop in at Plot Number-14, Bir Tikendrajit Marg, Chanakya Puri</p><p><strong>Cost for two:</strong> INR 500</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Assam Bhavan</strong></h4><p>Assam Bhavan’s Parampara Thali is an authentic assamese affair. To follow the organic welcome drink, you’re served <strong>Khar</strong> to cleanse your palate. Next up is <strong>Dal</strong>, <strong>Pitika</strong>, <strong>Duck Curry</strong>, <strong>fish cooked over charcoal and steamed in plaintain leaves</strong> and <strong>tangy Kahudi dry</strong>. To appease the sweet tooth, finish with rich <strong>Payesh</strong>.</p><p>Drop in at 1, Sardar Patel Marg, Chanakyapuri</p><p>Cost for two: INR 600</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Nagaland House</strong></h4><p>If you are a pork-lover, then this one you cannot afford to miss! A tiny restaurant, it is frequented by many for its<strong> pork ribs</strong> and <strong>Smoked Pork with Bamboo Shoots</strong>. Indian food, here we come.</p><p>Drop in at 29, Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Road, Opp. Race Course Metro Station</p><p>Cost for two: INR 350</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"></h4>
