2807	6 Marinades For Spicing Up Your Weekly Chicken Meals	<p>One of the simplest ways of flavouring food is to marinate it. The marinade itself could be extremely simple, or as complex as it gets, depending on the flavour notes that you&#8217;re trying to impart to your dish. Typically, the longer you let your food marinate, the more flavourful it is—since it soaks up all the juices like a sponge. Marinating works on all kinds of meat, as well as fish, cottage cheese, tofu, and vegetables.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Tandoori Yogurt Marinade</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">¾ cup plain yogurt</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 clove garlic, minced</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon ginger, grated</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon garam masala</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon paprika</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon black pepper</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Add all the ingredients to a bowl and firmly whisk together until you have a creamy paste. Your marinade is ready! Add your pieces of chicken (or anything else) to this bowl and set to refrigerate overnight for best results before cooking.</p><hr /><h4 style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Tangy-Apple-Thyme-Chicken.jpg"><img title = "6 Marinades For Spicing Up Your Weekly Chicken Meals  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2820" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Tangy-Apple-Thyme-Chicken.jpg" alt = "  " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Tangy-Apple-Thyme-Chicken.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Tangy-Apple-Thyme-Chicken-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Tangy-Apple-Thyme-Chicken-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></h4><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Tangy Apple Thyme Marinade</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons cider vinegar</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons apple juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons vegetable oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon grainy mustard or Dijon mustard</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon pepper</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Add all the ingredients to a bowl and firmly whisk together. Your marinade is ready! Place your pieces of chicken (or anything else) in the bowl and set to refrigerate overnight for best results before cooking.</p><hr /><h4 style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Chicken-Teriyaki.jpg"><img title = "6 Marinades For Spicing Up Your Weekly Chicken Meals  6 Marinades For Spicing Up Your Weekly Chicken Meals  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2817" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Chicken-Teriyaki.jpg" alt = "    " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Chicken-Teriyaki.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Chicken-Teriyaki-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Chicken-Teriyaki-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></h4><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Teriyaki Marinade</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 5 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup soy sauce</p><p style="text-align: center;">¾ cup water</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">5 tablespoon Mirin (Chinese sweet wine)</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon garlic powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon cornstarch</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>To prepare the marinade sauce, pour the soy sauce, water, sugar, and Mirin into a bowl and mix well. Add the chicken to this mixture and refrigerate for a minimum of 30 minutes. Make sure to leave some marinade for when you cook your teriyaki sauce.</p><p>When you begin your cooking, add the marinade sauce and garlic powder in a saucepan and mix well. Bring this to a boil and then add the cornstarch to the mixture and keep stirring until thickened, and your Teriyaki sauce is ready!</p><hr /><h4 style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Asian-Twist-Chicken.jpg"><img title = "6 Marinades For Spicing Up Your Weekly Chicken Meals  6 Marinades For Spicing Up Your Weekly Chicken Meals  6 Marinades For Spicing Up Your Weekly Chicken Meals  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2815" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Asian-Twist-Chicken.jpg" alt = "      " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Asian-Twist-Chicken.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Asian-Twist-Chicken-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Asian-Twist-Chicken-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></h4><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Best 4 Ingredient Chicken Marinade</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time: </strong>5 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup brown sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup soy sauce</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup vinegar</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Place all the ingredients in a bowl; make sure to add the brown sugar last so it can dissolve into the liquid.
