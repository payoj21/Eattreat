8217	Try Mughlai, Awadhi, & Parsi Stalls At The Jashn-e-Rekhta Food Festival	<p>The Urdu poetry festival, Jashn-e-Rekhta is coming to town this weekend, and we can&#8217;t help but be excited for the lovely line-up of events that their bringing. They also have the Rekhta Food Festival that&#8217;s going to happen within the main festival, and we&#8217;re telling you what you need to look out for.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>1. Ilham</strong></h4><p>Ilham is a catering service that serves traditional Afghani dishes to people in Delhi. It was launched in late 2015 by the UN refugee agency, UNHCR, and its partner Access. The initiative brings together seven women, all refugees from conflict-torn Afghanistan. The women make traditional Afghani food, and they’re coming to this edition of the Rekhta Food Festival with stories of their homeland in the form of food.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>2. Rumi&#8217;s Kitchen</strong></h4><p>A contemporary take on Awadhi cuisine, Rumi uses the flavours of Awadhi cuisine and interprets it in its own way without losing the essence. The menu is short, and the Biryani and Galauti Kebabs are their flagship products. Their signature desserts include the Shahi Tukra and Phirni.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>3. Bagli&#8217;s Kitchen</strong></h4><p>If you want to savour the traditional flavours of Parsi cuisine, this is where you should go. Get the dhansak, salli murghi, patra ni macchi and more from Bagli’s Kitchen. They’ve been catering for Parsi weddings for years and don’t have an outlet where you can just go on a whim to eat. This catering company is coming specially to the Rekhta Food festival.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>4. Imly</strong></h4><p><a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Katori_Chaat-Indian_Street_food.jpg"><img title = "Try Mughlai, Awadhi, &amp; Parsi Stalls At The Jashn-e-Rekhta Food Festival  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-8244" src="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Katori_Chaat-Indian_Street_food.jpg" alt = "restaurants food fest festival dinner Category:Restaurants   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Katori_Chaat-Indian_Street_food.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Katori_Chaat-Indian_Street_food-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Katori_Chaat-Indian_Street_food-768x460.jpg 768w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Katori_Chaat-Indian_Street_food-825x493.jpg 825w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Katori_Chaat-Indian_Street_food-410x246.jpg 410w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Katori_Chaat-Indian_Street_food-150x90.jpg 150w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p><p>They serve all our favourite street foods from across india under one roof, and they do it so well. They’re adding the quirk element to Rekhta with colourful chaats and spicy pani puris. Golgappa competition, anyone?</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>5. The Good Food Company</strong></h4><p>Rediscover classics and fall in love with perfectly crafted gourmet goodness from another world at The Good Food Company. Get food from the North West Frontier Region of Peshawar and gorge on some delectable meat dishes and biryanis while you’re here.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>6. Ahad Waza</strong></h4><p>They make Kashmiri wazwan, the traditional way. They’ve been upholding the Kashmiri way of cooking and serving food. Make your way over to their stall for some mouthwatering treats and delicacies from Kashmir.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>7. Biryani Blues</strong></h4><p><a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/biryani-blues.jpg"><img title = "Try Mughlai, Awadhi, &amp; Parsi Stalls At The Jashn-e-Rekhta Food Festival  Try Mughlai, Awadhi, &amp; Parsi Stalls At The Jashn-e-Rekhta Food Festival  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-8245" src="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/biryani-blues.jpg" alt = "restaurants food fest festival dinner Category:Restaurants   restaurants food fest festival dinner Category:Restaurants   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/biryani-blues.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/biryani-blues-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/biryani-blues-768x460.jpg 768w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/biryani-blues-825x493.jpg 825w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/biryani-blues-410x246.jpg 410w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/biryani-blues-150x90.jpg 150w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Biryani Blues</strong></em></p><p>One of the newer restaurants that serves the different kinds of biryani as their speciality, biryani Blues never disappoints in terms of quality and quantity.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>8. Punjab Grill</strong></h4><p>They’re coming to the festival too with their delicious Punjabi specialities. Tandoori Chicken is, after all, poetry for the senses too. Make sure you head to Punjab Grill for your dose of this tasty treat and more.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>9. Raju Da&#8217;s Singing Tree</strong></h4><p>This tea stall from CR Park is bringing all their happy vibes and 50 varieties of tea. Now you can sit and sip on your favourite, or try a new one while you enjoy the many performances and poetry sessions at the festival</p><p><strong>Head to</strong> Indira Gandhi National Centre for the Arts, 11 Mansingh Road, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Dates</strong> 17-19 Feb</p><p><strong>Time</strong> 4.30 pm &#8211; 9 pm</p><p><strong>Pay</strong> Entry free. <a href="http://www.jashnerekhta.org/register" target="_blank">Registration mandatory</a>.</p>
