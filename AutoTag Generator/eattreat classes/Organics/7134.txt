7134	One-Up Your Salad Game With This Greek Grilled Halloumi Recipe	<p>Bite-sized grilled cheese topped with a salad. Hey, it&#8217;s healthy if it has salad in it, okay? Grilled halloumi is the fun-est kind of cheese. Smoky, and&nbsp;super easy to prep, this Greek grilled halloumi recipe is one way of getting rid of that craving for cheese, without indulging too much.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 4-5</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups water</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup quinoa</p><p style="text-align: center;">60 gms mixed sprouts</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon lemon juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">200 gms slice halloumi cheese, grilled on each side for 4 mins on a grill pan</p><p style="text-align: center;">Ras El Hanout powder, for garnish</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Method</strong></h4><p>Add the water and salt to a pot and cook the quinoa for about 10-12 minutes. Transfer the quinoa to a large bowl, add in the mixed sprouts and lemon juice and toss. Set aside.</p><p>Place the grilled halloumi pieces on a serving plate, top with the quinoa and sprout mixture, sprinkle over the Ras El Hanout powder and serve!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <b>Wikimedia Commons</b></em></p>
