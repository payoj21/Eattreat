4655	#EatEffect // Green Falafel Healthy Wraps For Your Breakfast Blues	<p>We love these Green Falafel Healthy Wraps from Delectable Reveries. And of course, since we are all about eating and living healthy this week with #EatEffect, we thought of bringing you this delectable recipe.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>First Things First</strong></h4><p>Falafel is usually made with chickpeas and/or fave beans and fried into round balls or patties. They are served with pita bread or any flat bread, and hummus or tahini-based sauces.</p><p>I make my own version of falafel – with green lentils instead and adding other nutrient dense ingredients. I don’t know if we can even call this a falafel or it is simply a burger or patties. Whatever the name, I hope you’ll make them and enjoy them.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;">Elements:</h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups cooked green lentils</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 cup chopped walnuts</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/4 cup chickpea flour/gram flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/4 cup lemon juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 cloves of garlic</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tsp baking soda</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/4 cup olive oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tsp garam masala</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tsp cumin powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/4 tsp nutmeg powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/4 tsp cardamon powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup fresh cilantro</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 cup fresh mint</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tsp sea salt or more to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/4 cup flax seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tbs sesame seeds</p><div style="text-align: center;"><strong> Formula:</strong></div><p>Process all in above ingredients apart from flax and sesame seeds in a food processor till well combined and coarsely smooth.Add the flax and sesame seeds and pulse a couple of times.</p><p>Add the flax and sesame seeds and pulse a couple of times.</p><p>Spray Olive oil on a baking tray and drop dollops of the falafel mixture then flatten a little.</p><p>Spray the top surface of the falafels with some olive oil too.</p><p>Bake in a preheated oven at 350F / 180C for 12 to 15 minutes, then turn them over and bake for another 12 to 15 minutes.</p><p>These falafels are slightly crispy on the outside and soft inside.</p><p>I used Romaine lettuce as a wrap and served the falafels with avocado, sprouts, fresh mint and a yummy sauce.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">1 tbs almond butter</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tsp tahini</p><p style="text-align: center;">juice of half lemon</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tsp olive oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tsp agave</p><p style="text-align: center;">sea salt + black pepper</p><p style="text-align: center;">water</p><p>Blend it all together, taste, adjust, add more or not, be done. Enjoy!</p><hr /><p>This post first appeared on <a href="https://delectablereveries.com" target="_blank">Delectable Reveries</a> and is one in a series.
