This Carrot & Orange Soup Is For All The Winter Love	<p>Give those slender orange roots a chance and whip up this vibrant and healthy carrot<a href="http://eattreat.in/hearty-soups-to-warm-your-family-dinner-time-this-winter/"> soup recipe </a>this winter. The sweet and spicy combination of carrot, ginger and orange makes this carrot soup recipe a winning deal. Finally, add in some fresh orange juice and cream (if required) and voila your savoury and fruity bowl of warm carrot soup is ready to chomp!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 3</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time: </strong>20 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time: </strong>40 mins</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">500 grams carrots, thinly sliced</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 medium sized onion, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">5 garlic cloves</p><p style="text-align: center;">1-tablespoon fresh ginger</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 tablespoons olive oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 orange, juiced</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 teaspoons orange zest</p><p style="text-align: center;">5 cups vegetable broth</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 bay leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">Parsley, for garnish</p><p style="text-align: center;">A dollop of fresh cream, for garnish</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>In a saucepan, cook the carrots, onion and garlic and bay leaf on medium high heat for six to eight minutes, stirring occasionally.</p><p>Add the vegetable broth, orange zest and bay leaves and bring to a boil.</p><p>Now reduce the heat to medium low, cover the pan, and let it cook for 20 minutes, stirring in intervals. Remove from heat and allow this to cool.</p><p>Blend the soup in a food processor, in batches, until smooth and return all of it to the pan.</p><p>Stir in the orange juice and season with salt and pepper to taste.</p><p>Cook for another two minutes on medium heat. Serve hot and garnish with parsley and cream.</p>
