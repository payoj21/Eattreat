This Delicious Miso Broth With Shiitake & Tofu Is For You	<p>It can’t get better (and easier) than a warm and wholesome bowl of miso (fermented soy beans) broth with <a href="http://eattreat.in/soba-noodles-tofu-and-spring-onions-asian/">pok choy, udon noodles</a>, tofu and the umami punch of shiitake mushrooms. Grab some miso paste from your nearest supermarket and whip up the quintessential Japanese miso broth recipe from the comfort of your home in a matter of minutes!</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">250 grams soba noodles</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 bunches pok choy</p><p style="text-align: center;">5 cups vegetable broth</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons grated galangal</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup chopped scallions</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup tofu cubes</p><p style="text-align: center;">miso paste</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 cloves garlic, crushed</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup red miso</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formulae:</strong></h4><p>Heat the vegetable broth in a saucepan.</p><p>In a small bowl, mix 4 tablespoons of miso paste in some hot broth and add it back to the soup.</p><p>Add the ginger, scallions, garlic and shitake and let cook on low to medium low heat for 10 minutes.</p><p>Now add the pok choy and tofu and cook until the pok choy wilts.</p><p>Season with some white pepper to taste before pouring in the cooked Soba noodles.
