7136	This South Indian Millet Lemon Rice Is A Healthier Alternative To White Rice	<p>A healthier, high-fiber alternative to regular lemon rice. We love ourselves some tangy, yellow lemon rice, straight from a South Indian kitchen. But, unfortunately, those carbs can be a pain. Here&#8217;s the South Indian Millet Lemon Rice recipe which is just like the original, but also, HEALTHY!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2-3</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 5 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 20 mins</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups water</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup millet, thoroughly washed</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon mustard seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">1-inch ginger, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 tablespoons peanuts, roasted</p><p style="text-align: center;">2-3 dry red chillies, roasted</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 green chilli, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">2-3 curry leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">Juice of 2 limes</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 onion, chopped for garnish</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula</strong></h4><p>In a saucepan, add the water, salt and millet and cook on low heat for 10-12 minutes.</p><p>Heat the oil in a separate pan and cook the mustard seeds until they pop. Stir in the roasted peanuts and red chillies.</p><p>Now add in the chopped ginger, green chilli, curry leaves and turmeric powder and turn off the heat.</p><p>Mix in the cooked millet, add the lime juice and adjust salt to taste.</p><p>Garnish with the chopped onion and serve warm.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of:<b> Madishetty Manasa</b></em></p>
