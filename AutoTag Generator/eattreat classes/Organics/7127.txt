7127	A Classic Pumpkin Soup That Will Haunt Your Every Living Moment	<p>Who said we need Halloween to celebrate and <a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/winters-coming-4-restaurants-going-plenty-pumpkin/">savour them good old <i>kaddus</i></a>? Thick, rustic and creamy, this delicious easy pumpkin soup recipe makes a visit into our kitchen every once in a year (more like a customary fall pumpkin recipe)!</p><p>You can also spike up your<a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/fall-favorite-apple-ginger-pumpkin-soup-warm-fuzzies/"> simple pumpkin soup </a>with flavours like; leeks, apples, coconut milk or even bacon! Make sure to always use roasted pumpkins for your soupy preparations for optimum flavour.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Total time:</strong> 1 hour</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 8</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 leeks</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 large onions or 4 small onions</p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">½</span> teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 <span style="font-weight: 400;">½</span> tablespoons olive oil (6 teaspoons)</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 minced garlic cloves</p><p style="text-align: center;">1.6kg butternut pumpkin</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 litre chicken stock (4 cups) or 1 litre vegetable stock (4 cups)</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups water</p><p style="text-align: center;">cracked black pepper (to season)</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 pinch ground chilli powder (optional)</p><p style="text-align: center;">chopped fresh rosemary or parsley or coriander</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula</strong></h4><p>Wash the leeks under cold water for dirt and sand. Use only the white and light green parts.</p><p>Peel and chop the onions.</p><p>In a soup stock add olive oil, leeks, onions, garlic and salt, chilli powder, pepper. Cook and stir well for 5-6 minutes.</p><p>Now add the chicken stock and pumpkins. Boil this for approximately 20-25 minutes, or until the pumpkin is tender.</p><p>In a blender, puree the soup (the tender boiled vegetables). You can throw in some cashew nuts at this stage to make the puree creamier.</p><p>Reheat the puree with the leftover stock now.</p><p>Sprinkle some fresh chopped parsley for garnish.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Pixabay</strong></em></p>
