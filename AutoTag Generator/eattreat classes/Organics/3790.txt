3790	Some Pre-Diwali Health With Grilled Halloumi With Quinoa & Mixed Sprouts	<p>A recipe that you can prepare under 30 minutes for a quick, easy and wholesome meal. And if you have something big coming up, this is perfect to feel good about yourself without going on a diet.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 4-5</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups water</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup quinoa</p><p style="text-align: center;">60 grams mixed sprouts</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon lemon juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">200 grams sliced halloumi cheese, grilled on each side for 4 mins on a grill pan</p><p style="text-align: center;">Ras El Hanout powder, for garnish</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Add the water and salt to a pot and cook the quinoa for about 10 to 12 minutes.</p><p>Transfer the quinoa to a large bowl, add in the mixed sprouts and lemon juice and toss. Set aside.</p><p>Place the grilled halloumi pieces on a serving plate, top with the quinoa and sprout mixture, sprinkle the Ras El Hanout powder (North African spice mix) and serve!</p><p><em>Representative Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Pixabay</strong></em></p>
