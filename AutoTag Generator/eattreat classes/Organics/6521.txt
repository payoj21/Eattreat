6521	You Can Now Buy Super Speciality & Super Safe Spices From ITC MasterChef!	<p>ITC is now going to launch its range of speciality spices under the MasterChef brand that will go under tests on 450 parameters. ITC MasterChef really couldn&#8217;t get more thorough than that!</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>First Things First</strong></h4><p>All spices under ITC MasterChef have to go through tests for 450 chemicals, toxins, and other contaminants as opposed to only 9 parameters that are required to be tested in India. ITC is also planning to rule the fruits, vegetables, and seafood segment as well, and ITC Masterchef will most likely be the brand under which they sell it.</p><p>ITC is working very closely with farms for this particular project. Given the number of tests, these spices would undergo they would be extremely safe. In India, spices are only tested on 9 parameters, but these will be tested on 450 parameters.</p><p>The ITC Spice business, as such, is located in Guntur, Andhra Pradesh—which is also the chilli capital of the world. It is one of India’s leading producers and exporters of backward-integrated food safe spices and already engages with 7,800 spice farmers across 18,500 hectares and 210 villages.</p><p>As a whole, the diversified conglomerate has drawn up an overall investment plan of 25,000 crores. While they haven’t revealed how much they’ll be investing in each segment, they did say that there are about 65 projects going underway at the moment.</p><p>Considering the fact that people these days are becoming more health conscious and are trying their best to eat clean and live healthier lives, ITC is stepping up their game by focussing on the wellness aspect of their packaged foods. This will also help set them apart from their competitors.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>What&#8217;s More?</strong></h4><p>2016 saw the launch of some locally popular spices in the Telangana and Andhra Pradesh, under the ITC MasterChef brand, and now they’re planning to launch it on a national scale. There will be new product launches in this segment in 2017.</p><p>They are also planning to foray into the fruits, vegetables, and seafood business, and are looking at product categories like canned, frozen, dehydrated, etc. This may take about six to twelve months, and the launch may happen by early 2018. ITC MasterChef may become the umbrella brand for this as well.</p><p>At the moment, ITC is number three in the foods business in the country, but they’re looking to secure the top spot in the next few years.</p><p><em>Read the full story <a href="http://www.business-standard.com/article/companies/itc-to-launch-super-safe-spices-under-masterchef-brand-117010900292_1.html" target="_blank">here</a>.</em></p>
