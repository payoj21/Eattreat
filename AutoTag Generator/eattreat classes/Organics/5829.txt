5829	Hangover Remedies: Simple Fix-Its For Your Christmas Tizzy	<p>Festive season is upon us, and that means a whole lot of frolicking and drunken revelry. If you, like the rest of us mortals wake up after Christmas with a hangover that just doesn’t ebb, we urge you to try these fix-its. Hangover remedies to keep at your fingertips:</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Water</strong></h4><p>The elixir of life should be no.1 on your list of hangover remedies. Drinking water before heading to bed, can help prevent painful hangover symptoms. Grab some water in the morning too.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Ginger or Peppermint Tea</strong></h4><p>Turn to a <a href="http://eattreat.in/eateffect-eattreats-tea-detox-healthy/">soothing brew for reducing nausea </a>and motion sickness (both symptoms of a hangover).</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Coffee</strong></h4><p>The liquid in coffee can help rehydrate your body, and it does give you a <a href="http://eattreat.in/4-places-delhi-buy-roasted-coffee/">little boost of energy</a>. However, caffeine is a mild diuretic, so it could actually worsen the headache if taken excessively.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Fruit Juice</strong></h4><p>Pour yourself some juice the morning after all that revelry (one of our most useful hangover remedies). The fructose in sweet fruity drinks gives the body some instant energy, and juice also contains vitamins and water to help rehydrate the body.
