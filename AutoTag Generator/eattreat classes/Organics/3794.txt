3794	Lettuce Cups With Quinoa, Pineapple & Zucchini For Healthy Appetisers	<p>A healthy, delicious and filling appetiser for when you&#8217;re having friends over during the festive season.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 3-4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 20 mins</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">1 ½ cups water</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup quinoa</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ tablespoon oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ tablespoon butter</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cloves garlic, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ yellow zucchini, sliced</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup diced pineapples</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon cinnamon powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">5-6 baby lettuce leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">Balsamic vinegar, to taste</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Add the water and salt to a pot and cook the quinoa for about 10 to 12 minutes. Set aside.</p><p>Heat oil in a saucepan and add the butter, garlic, and the zucchini slices. Cook for about five minutes, until lightly browned.</p><p>Remove the zucchini from the pan, and set aside.</p><p>In the same pan, sauté the pineapples with cinnamon powder for two minutes.</p><p>Lay the lettuce on a plate, and add a spoon of the cooked quinoa on each leaf.</p><p>Place a slice of zucchini on each and top them with the pineapples. Add a drop or two of some balsamic vinegar and serve!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
