7125	This Sukhi Arbi With Amchur Is The Spicy Tangy Dish Your Life Needs	<p>A delicious dry arbi recipe! Arbi may not be the sexiest vegetable in town, but it sure gets the job done. Starchy and full of nutrients, we definitely love ourselves some health during the day. And that amchur powder adds the tang that your life and this dry arbi recipe needs.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2-3</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time: </strong>30 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time: </strong>30 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">250 grams arbi (colocasia root), half cooked in a pressure cooker, peeled and cut</p><p style="text-align: center;">Â½ teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon coriander powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon red chili powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Heat oil in a pan and add the pieces of arbi, shallow fry till the arbi begins to take on a golden brown color and set aside.</p><p>Heat oil in another pan or use the same one as before if you desire, add the ajwain seeds and let them fry for about a minute. Now incorporate the coriander and red chili powder, mix well. Place the fried arbi in this mixture and continue to mix well. Sprinkle in the amchur powder and salt, mix again and serve hot! You can garnish this sukhi arbi with some chopped mint leaves!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Pixabay</strong></em></p>
