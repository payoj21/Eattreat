7123	A Quick Healthy Amaranth Stir-Fry With A Heavenly South Indian Touch	<p>A stir-fry with one of the<a href="http://eattreat.in/green-leafy-vegetables-winter-guide/"> leafiest greens in town</a>! Amaranth is usually just what you need during the winters to stay healthy and yet experiment with your food. This stir fry is a sure-shot and quick way to a full and satisfying meal with amaranth recipes .</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 3-4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 15 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon mustard seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon urad dal (vigna mungo)</p><p style="text-align: center;">A few curry leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">4-5 red chilies, broken</p><p style="text-align: center;">4-5 cloves garlic, crushed</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 medium onion, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 bunch amaranth leaves, destemmed, thoroughly cleaned with water, shredded</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoon coconut, grated</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Heat oil in a pan and add the mustard seems, urad dal and curry leaves. Once the mustard seeds begin to splutter add in the red chilies and the crushed garlic. Give everything a good mix and add in the chopped onion, once they begin to soften add the amaranth leaves. Continue to stir; the leaves should start to shrink. Now add in the salt and grated coconut, stir everything once again before taking it off the heat. Serve with warm rotis or rice or even just a plain simple side!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
