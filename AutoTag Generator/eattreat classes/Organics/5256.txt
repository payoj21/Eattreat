5256	Nam, Meal & Ramen Bowls: Bowl Food Is Bowling Us Over	<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Change &#8211; It’s the only constant thing. Bowl foods are taking the world by storm and well, we have quite a bunch of restaurants doing the same in Delhi. Fatty Bao or Burma Burma will give you your share of ramen bowls and we can&#8217;t be comforted enough by it. </span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>First Things First</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Experts predict that the next food trend of 2017 has more to do with cutlery and tableware than the actual food. Baum + Whiteman, a well-known restaurant and food consultant based in New York, predicted that bowls will become the mainstream serving vessels.</span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Bowled Over</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This trend has been catching on in Pinterest and food blogs all over the world with the introduction of acai, poke, smoothie and Buddha bowls. Buddha Bowls, in particular, is very famous among the vegetarian community on Pinterest and YouTube. It basically consists of a “base of grains like quinoa and greens, topped with ingredients like sliced avocado, roasted vegetables like sweet potatoes or cauliflower, nuts and seeds”, says The Hindustan Times. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//this-pretty-instagram-trend-of-avocado-roses-has-us-swooning/">Smoothie bowls </a>are also highly photogenic and find many takers in the social media community. Rows of fresh cut fruits, topped with exotic nuts and seeds; smoothie bowls have been trendsetters on Instagram in recent times.</span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“What’s more, if you hold a bowl Buddha-like while eating, you are psychologically more prone to mindfulness about your meal,” the report by Baum + White mentions.</span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Celebrated food writer Nigella Lawson has dedicated an entire chapter to the beauty and health benefits of Bowled Foods in her cookbook “Simply Nigella”. “For me, ‘bowlfood’ is simply shorthand for food that is simultaneously soothing, bolstering, undemanding and sustaining,” she wrote.</span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This trend is accelerated by our continued obsession with Ramen bowls and the exotic <a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//thebiglist-23-essential-ingredients-for-your-asian-pantry/">Korean bibimbap</a>, which are served in the traditional, artisan style stone pots and bowls. So get your own bowl food, enjoy a healthy meal and don&#8217;t forget to take a picture!</span></p><p><em>Featured Photo Courtesy Of:<strong> Pixabay</strong></em></p>
