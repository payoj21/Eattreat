Here’s Our List Of New Superfoods You Have To Try	<p>If you’re one to troll for the latest health fads, then your news feed from last year was probably consumed with updates on kale, quinoa, acai berry bowls, and chia seeds. But while Instagram, Facebook and Pinterest are still abuzz with chia seed breakfasts, kale chips, kale smoothies, and quinoa salads; you’ll have to dig a little deeper to get ahead of the curve. Want to know what are superfoods? Read on to find more.</p><h4><strong>1. Activated Charcoal</strong></h4><p>Yes you heard right, and no you don’t need to<a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/gastronomica-activated-charcoal-spaghetti-recipe/"> head to the barbeque grill </a>and start gobbling chunks of coal. Activated charcoal has been used in Ayurveda and ancient Chinese medicine for donkey’s years because of its strong detoxification properties. This porous compound effectively binds toxins from your gastrointestinal tract and moves them to be excreted, hence reducing bloating, flatulence, and other digestive issues. If you’re too excited about popping it in your mouth, try adding it to your beauty routine to remove impurities from your skin and mouth. Protein content aside, it’s also loaded with vitamins A, B, and C (more than oranges), minerals, calcium and potassium. Often referred to as ‘<i>Peruvian Ginseng</i>’, Maca powder is unbelievably cool.&nbsp; It is your endocrine system’s best friend, stimulating your body to produce hormones and releasing the good ones into the bloodstream to regulate any hormonal imbalance in the body. It delivers rich doses of vitamin B ,C, and E, calcium, zinc, iron, magnesium, phosphorous and amino acids to your system. And if that wasn’t enough, this potent powder also boosts energy and improves the libido in both men and women.</p><h4><strong>4. Packed with all eight essential amino acids, it’s an ideal supplement for vegetarians, since it’s rich in over 12 vitamins, several minerals and a whole range of enzymes. Not just that; bee pollen sparks many of the body’s vital functions, boosting digestion, immunity and hormone levels. It’s also used as a stimulant because of it’s high levels of vitamin B, so you need to start mixing this wonder bug drug into your smoothies right away.</p><h4><strong>5. Matcha</strong></h4><p>This<a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/matcha-green-tea-tips-tricks/"> pretty little green powder</a> is super potent. Made from finely powdered green tea leaves, matcha is extremely rich in its chlorophyll content and has an extraordinarily high level of polyphenols, which have been tied to protection against heart disease and cancer.</p><p>For centuries Japanese Zen Buddhists have used it to induce a state of calm while remaining alert. What makes all of this possible? A high level of the amino acid L-Theanine, which promotes the production of alpha waves in the brain to induce relaxation without the inherent drowsiness caused by other downers. Expect to get Samurai strength and endurance after <a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/food-trends-2016/">drinking a matcha brew</a> because of its unique combination of high caffeine levels and L-Theanine. <a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/fatty-baos-ping-pong-sunday-brunch-in-delhi/">Matcha drinkers</a> experience clean energy for up to six hours from just one bowl, with none of the usual side-effects like nervousness and hypertension.</p>
