5618	A Secret Recipe Of A Quick Christmas Cake from the EatTreat Community!	<p>We got one of our community members, Pronomita Deka from <a href="http://eattreat.in/christmas-desserts-bakery-home-bakers/">Thekeikshop</a> to share her secret recipe for Quick Christmas Cake. This one’s an easy-peasy rum fruit cake recipe and doesn’t need too much preparation time.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup of mixed dry fruits(can use cranberries, candied cherries, prunes, currants, nuts)</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cup raisins (can use a combination of both golden and black)</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 cup dark rum (can use bourbon or Tia Maria or any coffee liqueur)</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup packed brown sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">175 gm (3/4 cup and 1/2 tablespoon) of unsalted butter</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 teaspoon nutmeg</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 large eggs</p><p style="text-align: center;">3/4 cups honey</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 1/2 cup all-purpose flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">4 tablespoons cocoa powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 teaspoon baking soda and 1/2 teaspoon of bicarbonate soda</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 teaspoon bicarbonate of soda</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon orange zest</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon vanilla extract</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula</strong></h4><p>Preheat oven to 150 degree Celsius.</p><p>Grease a 8 inch round pan with 3 1/2 inch deep with baking sheets both at bottom and sides. The baking sheets on the side have to be higher than the tin.</p><p>In a large bowl, put all the dry fruits and pour in the rum (or any alcohol of your choice), orange zest and spice; then add the butter, sugar, honey and Cocoa powder and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. Let it cool for 30 minutes.</p><p>Add the beaten eggs, flour, salt, baking soda and baking powder and combine well with a spatula or whisk.</p><p>Pour the batter into the prepared tin and bake for about 1 to 2 hours or until a skewer inserted comes out clean. Your Christmas Fruit Cake is ready!</p><p><strong>#EatExtra</strong> : Soak the dry fruits overnight in alcohol and spray the finished cake with the alcohol for a boozy Christmas cake.</p><p>You can follow <a href="https://www.facebook.com/thekeikshopdelhi/" target="_blank">Thekeikshop here</a>.</p><p><em>Photo used for representational purposes and courtesy of <strong>Pixabay</strong></em></p>
