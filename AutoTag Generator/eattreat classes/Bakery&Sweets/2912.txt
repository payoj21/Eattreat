2912	Fall Favorite: Pumpkin Halwa Instead Of Your Regular Treat	<p>As each day passes by, we can almost hear the rustle of the dry, golden coloured autumnal leaves, the sweet smell of warm spices in the air. Here&#8217;s how you can enjoy this beautiful season with this delicious, silky Indian dessert.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 4-5</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 20 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup ghee</p><p style="text-align: center;">800 grams pumpkin, finely grated</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup milk</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 ½ cups sweetened condensed milk</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon cardamom powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">A pinch of saffron, for garnishing</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Heat the ghee in a saucepan, add the grated pumpkin and sauté for about five minutes. Pour in the milk and continue to cook until the pumpkin is tender and almost dry.</p><p>Pour in the condensed milk and stir continuously until the mixture thickens. Sprinkle in the cardamom powder and mix well.</p><p>Garnish with saffron and serve hot!</p><p><em><strong>Photo courtesy of:</strong> Madishetty Manasa</em></p>
