3554	A Moist Almond Cake Is How We Celebrate The Nip In The Air	<p>Healthier than your usual flour and yummy to boot, we are loving this Moist Almond Cake. Maybe it&#8217;s us, but this sounds just perfect for a post-picnic meal.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves: </strong>5 &#8211; 6</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 30 mins</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">85 grams butter</p><p style="text-align: center;">130 grams sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">4 eggs</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup milk</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon vanilla</p><p style="text-align: center;">1½ cups almond flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup coconut flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 teaspoons baking powder</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Preheat your oven to 180 degrees Celsius. Grease a baking pan and set aside.</p><p>In a bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until smooth. Mix in the eggs, one at a time, beating continuously. Add in the milk and vanilla and mix until combined. Set this mixture aside.</p><p>In a separate bowl, combine together the flours, salt and baking powder.</p><p>Stir the dry ingredients into the wet ingredient mixture. Beat together until creamy.</p><p>Pour this mixture into the greased baking pan and bake for 30 minutes in the preheated oven. Remove from the oven, let cool for 10 minutes and serve!</p><p><em>Representative Photo Courtesy Of:<b> </b><strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p>
