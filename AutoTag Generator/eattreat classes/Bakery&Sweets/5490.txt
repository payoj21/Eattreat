5490	Old Delhi Desserts That Have Us Going Back For More	<p>Old Delhi, apart from other things, is known quite well for the plethora of North Indian and Mughlai food that you can find here—owing to the history attached to the area. One does not simply make a trip to Old Delhi without sampling at least one, if not all, of these.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Jalebis At Old Famous Jalebi Wala</strong></h4><p>We have to admit it. Our trip is incomplete without a visit to the Old Famous Jalebi Wala in Dariba Kalan. We always get a few pieces even if we’re full from stuffing our faces with all the gorgeous mutton dishes here.</p><p>Drop in at 795, Dariba Kalan, Opposite to Church, Chandni chowk</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Rabri Falooda At Giani Di Hatti</strong></h4><p>Rabri falooda is the best thing to cool down with on a hot day in the city. Get yours filled to the brim and in different flavours, at the very famous Giani di Hatti.</p><p>Drop in at Church Mission Road, Khari Baoli, Chandni Chowk</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Daulat Ki Chaat</strong></h4><p>As soon as you’ve stepped out of the Chawri Bazaar metro station, you’re greeted with hawkers selling Daulat ki Chaat in huge vessels, on their carts. This light, fluffy treat topped with pistachios melts in your mouth as soon as you take your first spoonful.</p><p>Drop in at Street carts on the intersection between Chawri bazaar and Nai Sarak</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Kheer At Bade Miyan Ki Kheer</strong></h4><p>The more we think about it, the more we feel that the doors of the Chawri Bazaar metro station open some sort of Pandora Box of food options. Bade Miyan ki Kheer is just a stone’s throw away from the metro station, and you can always find their loyal patrons lining up to get their daily dose of the dessert.</p><p>Drop in at Lal Kuan, Chawri Bazaar</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Phirni At Al-Jawahar</strong></h4><p>Al-Jawahar’s phirni wins, hands down. You cannot not end your meal at the restaurant with a cupful of their creamy, cold, delicious phirni.</p><p>Drop in at No.8, Jama Masjid Matia Mahal Road, Matia Mahal, Opp Gate No.1</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Kulfi At Kuremal Kulfiwale</strong></h4><p><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/12/15078724_10154082582283907_6398106314640607063_n.jpg"><img title = "Old Delhi Desserts That Have Us Going Back For More  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5530" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/12/15078724_10154082582283907_6398106314640607063_n.jpg" alt = "sweet old delhi iconic desserts Category:Bakery   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/15078724_10154082582283907_6398106314640607063_n.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/15078724_10154082582283907_6398106314640607063_n-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/15078724_10154082582283907_6398106314640607063_n-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <a href="https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10154082582283907&amp;set=a.275648718906.145571.532013906&amp;type=3&amp;theater" target="_blank"><strong>Shreyas Upasane</strong></a></em></p><p>Kuremal Kulfiwale has to be the most well-known place whenever one thinks of fond memories from one&#8217;s childhood spent in the streets of Old Delhi. They serve more than 60 varieties and even have stuffed kulfis on their menu!</p><p>Drop in at 526, Sitaram Bazar Rd, Kucha Pati Ram, Chawri Bazaar</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Fruit Cream At Deepak Diwan</strong></h4><p>Get copious amounts of soft, sweetened cream topped with assorted fruits like bananas, mangoes, pineapples, and apples with a large dollop of jam at the one of the only surviving ice cream cart vendor in this part of the city from Deepak Diwan. They’re quite well known for this and you can sweet talk your way into getting them to give you a little more of this sweet treat. He’s only in business after 5 pm, though.</p><p>Drop in at Deepak Diwan, Outside Gali Piyao Wali, Dariba Kalan</p><p>In case you&#8217;re stuck at work while you&#8217;re reading this, and we&#8217;re now the cause of your craving for any of these, you could get your favourite from <a href="http://www.olddelhifoods.com/home/" target="_blank">Old Delhi Foods</a>.</p><p><em>Featured Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p>
