7215	Move Over Gajar Ka Halwa! Try These Sweet Carrot Sesame Balls Instead	<p>Carrots in a sweet avatar. We&#8217;ve had enough of Gajar Ka Halwa (JK, we can never have enough) and here&#8217;s a new Carrot Sesame Balls Recipe that we&#8217;re trying this winter.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 5-6</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For the carrot filling</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">20 grams ghee</p><p style="text-align: center;">500 grams carrot, grated</p><p style="text-align: center;">250 ml milk</p><p style="text-align: center;">100 grams sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">10 grams cashew, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">10 grams raisins, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For the rice crust</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">100 grams rice flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">15 ml water</p><p style="text-align: center;">25 grams sesame seeds, roasted</p><p style="text-align: center;"><b></b><strong>Formula</strong></p><p><strong>For the carrot filling</strong></p><p>In a saucepan, add the ghee and the grated carrots and cook for 10 minutes on low heat. Now pour in the milk, add the sugar and dry fruits and continue to cook on low heat for about 45 minutes, stirring often, if not continuously. Set aside.</p><p><strong>For the rice crust</strong></p><p>In a bowl, add the water and the rice flour and mix together to obtain a doughy consistency. On a flat surface, knead this dough with your hands for about 10 minutes. Now evenly divide the dough into small balls (roughly an inch each). Flatten each dough ball to a thickness of about 2 cm.</p><p>Place about a tablespoon of the carrot filling on each piece of flattened rice dough, lift and stick together along the edges to form a ball.</p><p>Place these balls in a steamer, and steam for 10 minutes.</p><p>Remove from the steamer and roll them on some sesame seeds. Cool and serve!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of:Â <strong>Public Domain Pictures</strong></em></p>
