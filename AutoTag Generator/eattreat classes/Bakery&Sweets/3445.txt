3445	6 Authentic Bengali Sweet Shops In Delhi For Your Rosogollas & Chomchoms	<p>Here&#8217;s our list of the places in Delhi where you can find the yummiest, most authentic Bengali sweets for you to gift to those close to your heart. And now that a whole hoard of festivals are making their way into the calendar, these&nbsp;Bengali sweets in Delhi will be a life saver.&nbsp;</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Kamala Sweets</strong></h4><p>The markets in Chittaranjan Park are quite well known for the many eateries present there, and Kamala Sweets is one of the famous ones. The <strong>Jaffran Sandesh</strong>, <strong>Lord Chomchom</strong>, <strong>Nalen Gurer Sandesh</strong>, <strong>Maalpooah</strong> and <strong>Mishti Doi</strong> are delicious, and the chops, kachoris and <em>singaras </em>are definitely things you will love. You could also try the Labanga Latika, Doodhpuli (available only in winters), Orange Sandesh, Kamal Bhog, Cream Toast and Sharada Bhog.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong>&nbsp;K/1-101,&nbsp;Chittaranjan Park, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Say hello</strong> at&nbsp;011 26275476,011 41602577</p><p><strong>Pay</strong> INR 10/pc onwards</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Annapurna Sweets</strong></h4><p>Annapurna wins on both accounts—the locations in South Delhi, and the many different sweetmeats that they have to offer. The Mishti Doi, Kheer Kodom, Cham- Cham, and Roshokodom are par excellence. Annapurna originated in Chandni Chowk with the very first one in 1922. The regulars are loyal to their Chena Mithais — Rajbhog, Rasamalai, Shahi Toast. The Samosa&nbsp;is one of the bestsellers as people queue up to get their share. A must visit for those with a sweet tooth!</p><p><strong>Head to</strong> 10, DDA Market, Yusuf Sarai, Opposite Bharat Petroleum, Aurobindo Marg &amp; Shop No.38, Market No.2, Block J, Chittaranjan Park</p><p><strong>Say hello</strong> at&nbsp;011 26275198,011 32945717</p><p>Sweets starting at INR 180/kg</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Rasoraj</strong></h4><p>Rasoraj is also a popular sweet shop in Chittaranjan Park. They’re well known for their Gur Rosogulla, Cham-Cham, Kheer Kodom, Rajbhoj, Paneer ki Jalebi, Rabadi, and of course, the Mishti Doi. You can definitely count on Rasoraj for their authentic Bengali sweets.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong>&nbsp;D-767,&nbsp;Chittaranjan Park, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Say hello</strong> at&nbsp;+91 9350456422,+91 9311002073</p><p>Sweets starting at INR 15/pc</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Sandesh_-_Kolkata_2012-01-13_8319.jpg"><img title = "6 Authentic Bengali Sweet Shops In Delhi For Your Rosogollas &amp; Chomchoms  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3447" src="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Sandesh_-_Kolkata_2012-01-13_8319.jpg" alt = "sweets sweet shops South Ex New Delhi desserts CR Park New Delhi Category:Bakery bengali   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Sandesh_-_Kolkata_2012-01-13_8319.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Sandesh_-_Kolkata_2012-01-13_8319-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Sandesh_-_Kolkata_2012-01-13_8319-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></h4><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Bengali Sweet Centre</strong></h4><p>Located in the busy hub that is South Ex, Bengali Sweet Centre has its own dominion in the bustling market. While mithai is what they do best, their savoury snacks too have become well-known for their innovation and quality. They also have a chaat counter outside where you can satisfy your cravings for tangy and spicy snacks. Since it is so popular, it is always crowded even on non-festive days. Even so, the service is always quick and prompt. You should get the Badam Lauj, Pista Lauj, Badam Barfi, and Kalakand here.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong>&nbsp;G-19, Har Gyan Singh Arya Marg, South Extension I</p><p><strong>Say hello</strong> at&nbsp;011 24621022,011 24624202</p><p>Sweets starting at INR 34/plate</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Bangla Sweets</strong></h4><p>The Bengali sweets here are relished by people from all over Delhi. They have a huge variety of sweetmeats and savoury snacks, and their chaat is hugely popular too. Any time you’re in central Delhi, you should go here for their juicy Rosogollas, creamy Sandesh and cooling Rabri-Kulfi.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong>&nbsp;115-117, Gole Market, Bangla Saheb Marg, Connaught Place</p><p><strong>Say hello</strong> at&nbsp;011 23362257,011 23344371</p><p>Explore <a href="https://www.facebook.com/banglasweethouse/" target="_blank">Bangla Sweet House</a></p><h4><strong>Debnath Sweets &amp; Caterers</strong></h4><p>Tucked inside an inner lane of Mahavir Enclave, Debnath is where we head to when we are nearabouts Dwarka. Lot&#8217;s of Bengali families living in the area go to this shop purely out of the convenience, but we also love the&nbsp;cham cham,&nbsp;kheerkodoms,&nbsp;roshomalai&nbsp;and&nbsp;shondesh. Get here in the early hours of the morning and you&#8217;ll be able to catch hot&nbsp;kachori&nbsp;and vegetables, along with crispy runny jalebis. We have previously had these guys do small scale events and household family functions as well.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong>&nbsp;E-108, Bengali Colony Rd, Mahavir Enclave I, Mahavir Enclave, Delhi</p><p><strong>Say Hello</strong> at&nbsp;<span class="text ng-binding">+918750887606</span></p><p><em>Photos Courtesy Of: <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p>
