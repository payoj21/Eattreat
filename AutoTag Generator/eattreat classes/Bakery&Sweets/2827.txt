2827	Fall Favourite: Classic Carrot Cake With Cream Cheese Frosting	<p>Fall weather is almost here and we&#8217;re all set to swing our spatulas for some intense baking sessions! We bring to you our favorite recipe of the delicious Classic Carrot Cake with a Cream Cheese Frosting. The strong flavours of cinnamon and nutmeg along with freshly shredded carrot makes this one a winner. Preparing this was mad fun. The only tedious bit was the shredding of carrots but then the end result makes each step seem like a joy ride. Bake away!</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup (200 grams) light brown sugar, packed (or dark brown sugar)</p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">¾</span> cup (180 ml) vegetable oil</p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">¼</span> cup (60 grams) yogurt</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 large eggs</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 teaspoons vanilla extract</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups (250 grams) all-purpose flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon baking soda</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 teaspoons ground cinnamon</p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">¼</span> teaspoon ground nutmeg</p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">½</span> teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups (260 grams) very finely grated carrots</p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">½</span> cup walnuts</p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">½</span> cup raisins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For The Frosting</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups cream cheese</p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">¼</span> cup unsalted butter</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon vanilla</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 <span style="font-weight: 400;">½</span> cups powdered sugar</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Preheat oven to 350˚F degrees. Grease a springform pan and keep aside.</p><p>In a large bowl combine brown sugar and oil. Beat in the yogurt until fully combined &#8211; about 60 seconds. The mixture will be gritty and thick. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Now mix in the vanilla.</p><p>In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. With a spatula, manually stir the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients until just combined and all flour pockets are gone — do not overmix!</p><p>Fold in the finely shredded carrots, raisins, and walnuts. Pour into the prepared springform pan</p><p>Bake for 32-38 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Do not overbake, or it will dry it out. Check the cake at 30 minutes, then again at the 32-minute mark. Our cake took exactly 34 minutes. Allow cake to cool completely before frosting.</p><p><strong>For The Frosting</strong></p><p>Beat cream cheese, butter and vanilla in a large bowl with a mixer until blended. Add sugar in installments, beating after each addition.</p><p>Now smear the frosting on your yummy cake!</p><p><em><strong>Photo Courtesy Of:</strong> <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/anniepancake/12640419183/">AnnieAnniePancake</a> via <a href="https://visualhunt.com/photos/coffee-images/">Visual hunt</a></em></p>
