3471	Navratri Sweets That You Must Indulge In This Festive Season	<p><b></b>While Navratri’s are here, we are off all kinds of savoury things (how much we miss the chicken wings) but it’s a time when we can dwell on sugary sweets instead! So, here is a list of mithai’s you should be gorging on before the festive season is over.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>1. Sev Badam at Chaina Ram </strong></h4><p>Chaina Ram in Chandni Chowk is our all-time Old Delhi favourite and their sweets are something no festive season is complete without. Loaded with almonds and pistachios, <strong>Sev Badam</strong> melts in the mouth.</p><p>Explore Chaina Ram <a href="http://chainaram.com" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><p><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Kalakand_of_Salem.jpg"><img title = "Navratri Sweets That You Must Indulge In This Festive Season  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3492" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Kalakand_of_Salem.jpg" alt = "sweet old delhi iconic desserts   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Kalakand_of_Salem.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Kalakand_of_Salem-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Kalakand_of_Salem-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>2. Milk Cake at Gopala</strong></h4><p>Gopala has a tonne of delicious desserts, including their large-sized <strong>rasgullas</strong> but if you are fasting during Navratri, it’s the <strong>milk cake</strong> you can relish without guilt. Grainy and sweet, it is like an Indian version of decadent, caramelised fudge cake.</p><p>Explore Gopala <a href="http://gopala.in">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>3. Kesar Rasmalai at Standard Burfee </strong></h4><p>This milky dessert is creamy and sweet. Even though it is of Bengali origin, it remains one of the most celebrated desserts during Navratri, owing to its cold temperature. The addition of Kesar just takes it up a notch.</p><p>Explore Standard Burfee <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Standard-Burfee/432010320169109" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>4. Coconut Burfi at Bharat Sweet House</strong></h4><p>If you are a coconut fan (or not) you ought to fall in love with this two-coloured unique piece of mithai. Served in rectangles and wrapped in Bharat Sweet butter paper individually, it is a treat for the taste buds.</p><p>Explore Bharat Sweet House <a href="http://www.bharatsweethouse.com">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>5. Milk Mysore Pak at Sarvana Bhawan </strong></h4><p>A South Indian delicacy, <strong>Mysore Pak</strong> tastes as decadent as a fudge but has a smooth texture. Made with generous amounts of ghee, milk, cardamom and sugar, this rich dessert is absolutely delicious and quite sweet.</p><p>Explore Sarvana Bhawan <a href="http://www.saravanabhavan.com" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>6. Kulfi at Ravi Raj </strong></h4><p>Everyone fasting during Navratri will agree that kulfi is must have after the heavy “fasting” meal and no one does stick kulfi better than Ravi Raj. Available in different flavours, its signature badam kulfi is our all time favourite.</p><p>Explore Ravi Raj <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Raviraj-Di-Kulfi/224982610881507" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>7. Motichoor Laddoo at Tewari Brothers </strong></h4><p>Laddoo is an all-time puja classic. Get your batch of laddoos from Tewari Brothers in Chandi Chowk. An establishment which began its journey six decades ago, is now known for pure desi ghee products and their <strong>Motichoor Laddoos</strong> are what keep us coming back!</p><p>Explore Tewari Brothers <a href="http://tewaribrossweets.com/contact_us.html" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>8. </strong>Dhoda<strong> at Om Sweets</strong></h4><p>A sticky sweet slice enriched with walnuts and almonds, <strong>Dhoda</strong> is a traditional Punjabi dessert. Our personal favourite and entrusted for years, Om Sweets is the go-to place for some good quality Dhoda.</p><p>Explore Om Sweets <a href="http://www.omsweets.in" target="_blank">here</a>.</p>
