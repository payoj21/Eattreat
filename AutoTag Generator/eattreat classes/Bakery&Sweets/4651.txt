4651	There Is A Reason We Love Cheese Fondue For Filling Chilly Nights	<p>Something about the chilly air makes us reach for warm things and what’s better than a piping hot bowl of cheese. Dip in bread, potatoes, vegetables or meat for cheese fondue makes everything better! Try this pungent cheese fondue for your guests or a romantic evening. (But cheese is bae, so make sure you compensate in other ways!)</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements: </strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">1 and 1/2 cups Swiss cheese</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups Gruyere cheese</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 cup Grana Padano</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tbs all-purpose flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 clove garlic, crushed</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup dry Sauvignon Blanc or any other dry white wine</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tbs lemon juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 tbs Kirschwasser</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>In a bowl, mix together the chosen cheeses and all-purpose flour, until it coats the grated cheese evenly.</p><p>In a heavy saucepan add garlic and wine. Reduce the heat to low and just before the mixture gets to boil, add lemon juice.</p><p>Keeping the heat on low, add the cheese mixture slowly and in batches. Stir continuously, ensuring the added cheese is melted before mixing in another batch.</p><p>Add Kirschwasser and keep on simmer setting.</p><p>Serve while maintaining the heat in a fondue pan to make sure it stays creamy and smooth as you eat! This cheese fondue is a winner.</p><p><em>Featured Photo Courtesy Of: <a href="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f9/Cheese_fondue_Rokko_Kobe01n3200.jpg" target="_blank"><strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></a></em></p>
