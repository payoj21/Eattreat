4731	Travel The World With These 5 World Cuisine Restaurants In Mumbai	<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Have you ever wanted to backpack on an impulse and visit different countries <a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//culture-travel-in-india-food/">just to try out their food </a>? But how lucky Mumbaikars are when plenty of world cuisine restaurants are ready to offer what our heart truly wants! The two G’s &#8211; Good and Global come together here!</span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Heng Bok for Korean</strong></h4><p>Korean food though may not be on your bucket list but this restaurant surely serves some excellent <strong>Kochuzang Sangyeop</strong>, which is eaten like a <strong>Pan</strong> and <strong>Bulgogi Deopbap</strong>, a sticky bowl rice preparation with thinly sliced marinated beef. Gobble all the meaty goodness with a glass of <strong>Jinro Soju</strong>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Green Onion for Nigerian</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">One can find the Nigerian staples like <strong>Prawn Egusi</strong> (A curry made of dried fish and melon seeds), Nigerian <strong>tomato based fish stew</strong> and Nigerian<strong> goat jollof rice</strong>. Ruby Dogra, the owner of green onions, says that the Nigerian ladies who visited the nearby hospitals shared their recipes with her which lead to the beginning of this restaurant.</span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Doppio for Vietnamese</strong></h4><p><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/11/14940023_1745999542391967_4354193073663029931_o.jpg"><img title = "Travel The World With These 5 World Cuisine Restaurants In Mumbai  "class="alignnone wp-image-4753 size-full" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/11/14940023_1745999542391967_4354193073663029931_o.jpg" alt = "restaurants mumbai global Chinese Category:Restaurants asian american   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/14940023_1745999542391967_4354193073663029931_o.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/14940023_1745999542391967_4354193073663029931_o-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/14940023_1745999542391967_4354193073663029931_o-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Doppio</strong></em></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Doppio has added south-east influence to its new dishes, serving authentic Vietnamese <strong>rice paper wraps</strong> with <strong>pickled carrot and papaya</strong>, <strong>Hoisin tiger shrimp</strong> with <strong>wasabi mash</strong> and sautéed leaks are some of the mouthwatering dishes that are hard to find anywhere in Mumbai! Even though they have a very exhaustive world cuisine menu.</span></p><p>Explore more <a href="https://www.facebook.com/doppiomumbai/?fref=ts" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Stacks and Racks for American</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Love combination of bacon, cheddar and biting into juicy perfectly grilled tender meat?
