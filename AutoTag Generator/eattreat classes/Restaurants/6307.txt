6307	Gastronomica’s Activated Charcoal Spaghetti Is The Recipe To A Gothic Romance	<p>Every time we make our way to Gastronomica, we have to try this unusual combination of charcoal and spaghetti. Infant they do a lot of<a href="http://eattreat.in/charcoal-martini-gastronomica-delhi/"> black and charcoal-themed eats </a>and we must say, they are absolutely intriguing. This Gastronomica Activated Charcoal Spaghetti recipe comes straight from the kitchen and bar for our beloved Eattreaters.</p><p>So are you ready to roll?</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">1 package spaghetti</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons olive or vegetable oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 bellpeppers, sliced (1/4 cup)</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 medium onion, diced (1/2 cup)</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 medium zucchini, cut into 1/2-inch slices (4 cups)</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 garlic cloves, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 medium tomatoes, cut into 1-inch pieces</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup frozen green peas, thawed</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon chopped fresh basil leaves or 1 teaspoon dried basil leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon pepper</p><p style="text-align: center;">2/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup Charcoal liquid</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula</strong></h4><p>Cook and drain spaghetti as directed on package.</p><p>While spaghetti is cooking, heat oil in 10-inch skillet over medium-high heat. Cook bellpeppers, onion, zucchini and garlic in oil, stirring frequently, until vegetables are crisp-tender.</p><p>Stir in remaining ingredients except cheese; cook until hot.</p><p>Serve vegetable mixture over spaghetti. Sprinkle with cheese.</p>
