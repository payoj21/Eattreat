3796	A Different Kind Of Bong High: CR Park Style Chingri Prawns Bruschetta	<p>This rustic and creamy prawn starter is inspired from Bengali cuisine with a modern twist!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook time:</strong> 15 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">250 grams chingri prawns, deveined and heads separated</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For the creamy prawns</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">Separated prawn heads</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tomato, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1-tablespoon mango-ginger, grated</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ onion, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cloves garlic, minced</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ tablespoon coriander powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ tablespoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ tablespoon red chilli powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ gondhoraj lime, juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ kafir lime, juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For the prawn topping</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">Oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ tablespoon mango ginger</p><p style="text-align: center;">Butter</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For the toast</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">1 baguette</p><p style="text-align: center;">Olive oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">Sea salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">Freshly ground pepper</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Method:</strong></h4><p><strong>For the creamy prawn sauce</strong></p><p>In a medium skillet add <span style="font-weight: 400;">¾</span> onion and sauté till it gets translucent in colour.</p><p>Now add ½ tablespoon mango ginger and garlic and sauté further.</p><p>Once the ginger and garlic start imparting its aroma, add the prawn heads, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chilli powder and stir this well for 1 minute.</p><p>Now slowly stir in the coconut cream and salt. Let this cook on medium heat for 3-4 minutes.</p><p>Remove from heat, stir in the lime juice. Strain the sauce well using a sieve.</p><p><strong>For the toast</strong></p><p>Preheat oven at 200 degree. Slice the baguette into bruschetta size toasts.</p><p>Drizzle some olive oil, pepper and salt. Toast these for roughly 20 minutes, turning the side after 10 minutes.</p><p>Now place the toast in a serving plate and top it up with the prepared creamy prawns. Garnish the bruschetta with microgreen coriander and pea shoots.</p><p><strong>For the prawn topping</strong></p><p>Heat some oil in a medium size skillet. Add the rest of the onions and ½ tablespoon mango ginger and sauté well.</p><p>When it begins to sizzle, add prawns, butter and the creamy prawn sauce. Cook this on medium heat for 2-3 minutes on medium heat. Check for seasoning once done.</p><p>Now place the toast on a serving plate and top it up with the prepared prawns. Garnish the bruschetta with micro green coriander and pea shoots.</p><p><em>Representative Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p>
