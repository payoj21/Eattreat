2616	5 Easy But Genius Ice Cubes You Should Be Making At Home	<p>Summers aren&#8217;t leaving and with the little bouts of rain and sun, you&#8217;ll want something to keep you cool. Try these inventive ice cubes to make your drinks entertaining and refreshing.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong> 1. Lemon and Mint Ice Cubes</strong></h4><p>Making mojito? (Virgin or not, we aren&#8217;t judging!) The usual ice cubes dilute the flavour, so try these lemon and mint cubes to sass up your drink.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Take out those baking trays you haven&#8217;t used in a while. Cut lemons or limes in thin spherical shapes, toss in a few mint leaves, pour water and freeze. Add these to the glasses, for a perfect look and relish.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>2. Coffee Cubes </strong></h4><p>If you run on iced coffee, you know the pain of reaching the fag end and having to drink just coffee-water (that&#8217;s not iced coffee!).</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula: </strong></p><p>Freeze hot coffee in ice cube trays and add them to your glass when needed. Voila! You have a perfect glass of iced coffee.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>3. Fruit Ice Cubes</strong></h4><p>Want to serve some fresh juice or maybe add a fruity flavour to your cocktails. Fruit cubes is the way to go! Even if you&#8217;re drinking plain water, infusing them with these fruit cubes is a good idea for keeping your system hydrated.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Juice whatever fruit you like and freeze, add to drinks and let the party begin! Crush the ice cubes in a food processor for a quick and trouble-free granita.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>4. Smoothie Cubes</strong></h4><p>Making smoothies or got some fruit leftover? Freeze them in cubes. Efficient and easy, they can be stored in ziplock bags once frozen.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Simply throw peeled fruits in a blender and once they reach a smooth consistency, freeze.</p><p><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Rosé_dété.jpg"><img title = "5 Easy But Genius Ice Cubes You Should Be Making At Home  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2618" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Rosé_dété.jpg" alt = "  " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Rosé_dété.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Rosé_dété-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Rosé_dété-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Courtesy Of: <strong><a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Rosé_d%27été.jpg" target="_blank">Wikimedia</a></strong></em></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>5. Champagne/Wine Cubes</strong></h4><p>Got some of last night&#8217;s champagne left over? Cube it! Add orange or grapefruit juice in the morning to make mimosas.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Freeze the champagne! Add orange rinds to take it a notch higher.</p><p>Get creative and start freezing!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of:<strong><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/simpleprovisions/15800334210/in/photostream/" target="_blank"> Amelia Crook</a></strong></em></p>
