3375	Booze Upgrade: A Desi Punch Recipe For Your Next House Party	<p>The festive season in Delhi is in full swing, and we&#8217;re super excited for pandal hopping, Ram Leela, gorging on delicious food, garba, dandiya, and all the fun parties that are about to ensue. Here&#8217;s a punch recipe for you desi peeps to add that extra &#8216;high&#8217; to lift up your spirits when you host your next soiree!</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">4 bottles vodka</p><p style="text-align: center;">8 litres water</p><p style="text-align: center;">A 20 litre Bisleri bottle</p><p style="text-align: center;">A dispenser</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For the spicy mint and coriander paste</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">500 gms mint leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">250 gms coriander leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">Black salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">20 green chillies</p><p style="text-align: center;">8 heaped tablespoons dried mango powder (<em>amchur</em>)</p><p style="text-align: center;">4 tablespoons asafoetida (<em>hing</em>)</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For the tamarind chutney</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">400 grams seedless tamarind</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 litre water</p><p style="text-align: center;">100 grams cumin powder (<em>jeera</em>)</p><p style="text-align: center;">100 grams ginger powder (<em>saunth</em>)</p><p style="text-align: center;">50 grams asafoetida</p><p style="text-align: center;">50 grams chilli powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">750 grams jaggery</p><p style="text-align: center;">Oil for cooking</p><p style="text-align: center;">Rock salt/ black salt as required</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>For the sugar syrup</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">1 litre water</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 litre sugar</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p><strong>For the mint and coriander paste:</strong></p><p>Grind together mint, coriander, green chillies, <em>amchur</em>, salt, black salt, asafoetida.</p><p>Add water to grind to a fine paste.</p><p><strong>For the tamarind chutney:</strong></p><p>Soak tamarind in water for 4-5 hours.</p><p>Squeeze pulp, strain and keep aside.</p><p>Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, let it crackle.</p><p>Put the pan on simmer. Add ginger powder, red chilli powder and cook for 7-10 minutes.</p><p>Add the jaggery and salt. Cook for another 4-5 minutes.</p><p>Remove pan from stove once done. Cool and store in an airtight container.</p><p><strong>For the sugar syrup:</strong></p><p>In an aluminium bottomed vessel, boil sugar and water together till it becomes syrupy.</p><p>Cool and set aside.</p><p><strong>For the punch:</strong></p><p>In a 20l Bisleri bottle, mix 8 liters of cold water to the mint and coriander paste and mix well. Add the tamarind chutney and the sugar syrup.</p><p>Add the vodka and mix well.</p><p>Completely fill the dispenser with ice cubes and mint leaves. Set the bottle on top, and serve!</p><p><strong>#EatExtra:</strong> Have your glasses rimmed with a wedge of lemon, and dip it in granulated sugar before filling it up with your drink. Additionally, you could also fill <em>golgappas</em> with a little mix of boiled chick peas and potatoes and put it on top to make it look a little fancy.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p>
