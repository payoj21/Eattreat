2317	What Cheeses Are You Pairing With Your Wine? Here’s A Handy Guide	<p><a href="http://eattreat.in/pair-pizza-wine-match-made-heaven/">Wine and cheese pairings</a> are as legendary a duo as Bonnie and Clyde. But if your wine and cheese pairings go off, your dinner party can end as tragically as their love story did. Mansi Jasani of The Cheese Collective takes some time away from her love affair with cheese to ensure that nothing comes between this delicious union.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>First Things First</strong></h4><p>If you’re unfamiliar with <a href="https://www.facebook.com/TheCheeseCollective/?fref=ts" target="_blank">The Cheese Collective</a>, it’s time you were acquainted. Naturally, we at EatTreat like imported cheeses as much as the next person—ok, maybe even a teeny bit more—but these guys make and curate the dairy delights from around the country (they have a curry leaf cheese!), and are determined to put India on the global cheese map. We picked their brain on wine and cheese pairings, and it’s time you got in on the intel.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Fresh &amp; Soft</strong></span></h4><p style="text-align: center;">To get started, softer cheeses go with light, fruity wines.</p><p><strong>Cheese: </strong>Chèvre, Feta, Brie, Camembert</p><p><strong>Wine: </strong>Champagne, Riesling, Moscato, Chenin Blanc, Sauvignon Blanc, Rosé, Lambrusco, Cava</p><p><strong>An Indian Pick: </strong>Charosa Selections Sauvignon Blanc</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Semi Hard</strong></span></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Semi-hard cheeses are usually perfectly paired with medium-bodied ones.</p><p><strong>Cheese: </strong>Emmental, Gruyère, young Cheddar</p><p><strong>Wine: </strong>Chardonnay, Viognier, Champagne, Pinot Noir, Zinfandel, Merlot</p><p><strong>An Indian Pick: </strong>Grover&#8217;s Viognier</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Hard</strong></span></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Dense, aged cheeses are in a party with complex wines.</p><p><strong>Cheese: </strong>Aged Gruyère, Grana Padano, Pecorino, Manchego, Parmigiano Reggiano</p><p><strong>Wine: </strong>Bordeaux, Cabernet Sauvignon, Barolo</p><p><strong>An Indian Pick: </strong>Indus Cabernet Sauvignon</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Blue</strong></span></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Blue cheese goes with dessert wines.</p><p><strong>Cheese: </strong>Stilton, Gorgonzola, Roquefort</p><p><strong>Wine: </strong>Port, Sauternes, Dessert wines</p><p><strong>An Indian Pick: </strong>Bellisimo by Big Banyan</p><p>If that’s still too much to digest, just play it safe with a sparkling wine–Champagne and Prosecco—that goes perfectly with just about any variant.</p>
