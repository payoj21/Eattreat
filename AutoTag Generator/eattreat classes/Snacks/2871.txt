2871	Oats Uttapam With Roasted Coconut Chutney For A Healthy Brekkie	<p>A healthy rendition of the classic uttapam with oats, and a delicious roasted coconut chutney!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 4 hours</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 20 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Oats Uttapam</strong></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">½ cup oats</span><span class="s1"><br /></span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">½ cup semolina<br /></span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">½ cup yoghurt<br /></span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">½ teaspoon mustard seeds</span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">½ teaspoon cumin seeds<br /></span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">1 ¼ cup water<br /></span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">½ onion, finely chopped</span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">2-3 spring onions, finely chopped<br /></span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">1 tomato, chopped</span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">Green chillies<br /></span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">Oil<br /></span></p><p class="p1" style="text-align: center;"><span class="s1">Salt, to taste</span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Roasted Coconut Chutney</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup fresh coconut</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon chana dal (split baby chickpeas) – wash and soak for 4 hours prior to preparation</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon raw peanuts</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 green chillies</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon ginger</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon mustard seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon cumin seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">Pinch of asafoetida (<em>hing</em>)</p><p style="text-align: center;">2-3 dried red chillies</p><p style="text-align: center;">6-7 curry leaves</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p><strong>Oats Uttapam</strong></p><p>Get started with blending the oats in a blender, grind until oats turn into a fine powder. Transfer to a bowl.</p><p>Add the semolina, yoghurt, mustard, and cumin seeds to the powdered oats. Mix everything up and add water while stirring it into a batter, keep adding water to get the right consistency. Your batter should be smooth with no lumps.</p><p>Add the chopped onions, tomatoes, spring onions and chillies. Throw in salt to taste and mix it all up.</p><p>Heat pan over medium heat with oil and add the batter to the pan in small portions to get a pancake-like shape, turn over so the other side can cook.</p><p><strong>Roasted Coconut Chutney</strong></p><p>Mix coconut, soaked chana dal, peanuts, green chillies, salt and sugar to taste with some water in a blender and grind until you get a smooth paste.</p><p>Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida (<em>hing</em>), dried red chillies and curry leaves.</p><p>Pour over coconut chutney and serve with Oats Uttapam!</p><p><em>Photos Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
