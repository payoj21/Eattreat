5917	Head To These 7 Places For Your Authentic Burmese Khao Suey Cravings	<p>A traditional dish that came to East India during World War 2, Burmese khao suey has found itself becoming a popular choice among Delhiites who want to (literally) mix it up at the dinner table. Every restaurant does Khao Suey differently, so we’ve curated a list of Khao Suey serving places for you to sample.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Burma Burma</strong></h4><p>The <strong>Burma Burma Oh No Khow Suey</strong> is their signature bowl of coconut milk with veggies served with an array of toppings. You can choose from Udon, Hakka, Whole Wheat and Flat Noodles.</p><p>Head to DLF Cyber Hub, Shop No 6 , Building No 8</p><p><strong>Cost:</strong> INR 1,500 for two</p><p>Follow them <a href="https://www.facebook.com/BurmaBurmaIndia/?fref=ts" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"></h4><h4 style="text-align: center;"></h4><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Wok In the Clouds</strong></h4><p>Wok in The Clouds is a multi-cuisine restaurant that specialises in variants of Burmese khao suey. Must-tries here are their <strong>Gaeng Kiew Kai</strong> and <strong>Khaeng Phat Kai</strong> which are a twist on the vintage khao suey. These have spicy coconut milk broths with rice, veggies and chunks of perfectly cooked chicken in between.</p><p>Head to Shop 52, Khan Market, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Cost:</strong><em> </em>INR 600</p><p>Follow them <a href="https://www.facebook.com/WokintheClouds.KhanMarket/?fref=ts" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Ping’s Café Orient</strong></h4><p>With a décor that enthrals, and food that floors, Ping’s Café Orient is a must visit. They’re known for their Asian and Burmese flavours and the Burmese khao suey is fab. With veggie chunks, creamy broth and mildly flavoured noodles, you would want to come back again and again.</p><p>Head to 13, Main Market, Lodhi Colony, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Cost:</strong> INR 600</p><p>Follow them <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pingsbypco/?fref=ts" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Piali – The Curry Bistro</strong></h4><p><a href="http://eattreat.in/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/14590010_1057216871064079_6210451392967132615_o.jpg"><img title = "Head To These 7 Places For Your Authentic Burmese Khao Suey Cravings  "class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5989" src="http://eattreat.in/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/14590010_1057216871064079_6210451392967132615_o.jpg" alt = "noodles khow suey delhi Category:Snacks burmese   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/14590010_1057216871064079_6210451392967132615_o.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/14590010_1057216871064079_6210451392967132615_o-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/14590010_1057216871064079_6210451392967132615_o-768x460.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Piali</strong></em></p><p>The piece de resistance in any khao suey is the broth – and Piali does it just right. A perfect balance of sweet and sour, the broth is accompanied with lightly flavoured noodles that make the dish a sure winner.</p><p>Head to K 41, Level 1, Opposite PVR Plaza, Connaught Place, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Cost:</strong> INR 400</p><p>Follow them <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pialicp/" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Berco’s</strong></h4><p>People remember this place from years back, and surely, it has been around for ages. We’re sure you’ve tried a lot off their menu, but have you tried their khao suey? A truly lip smacking affair, the star of the dish are the spices at Berco’s. Don’t miss this one. It’s easy on the pocket too.</p><p>Head to G-2/43, Middle Circle, Connaught Place, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Cost:</strong> INR 350</p><p>Follow them <a href="https://www.facebook.com/BercosIndia/?fref=ts" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Mamagoto</strong></h4><p>Mamagoto is known for their burst of flavours. Truly, every dish whipped out by their kitchen is a zesty, flavoursome affair. Their<strong> Chicken Khow Suey</strong> is a popular dish. With real flavours, home grown spices, juicy chicken bits and fresh veggies, it’s a stunner.</p><p>Head to 53, 1<sup>st</sup> floor, Middle Lane, Khan Market, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Cost:</strong> INR 500</p><p>Follow them <a href="https://www.facebook.com/MamagotoFunAsian/?fref=ts" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Farzi Café</strong></h4><p>If you fancy khao suey with a twist, try the Pumpkin and Coconut Khow Suey at Farzi Café. You will not be disappointed.</p><p>Head to E Block, Connaught Place, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Cost:</strong> INR 485</p><p>Follow them <a href="https://www.facebook.com/FarziCafeCP/" target="_blank">here</a>.</p><p><em>Featured Image Courtesy Of: <strong>Burma Burma</strong></em></p>
