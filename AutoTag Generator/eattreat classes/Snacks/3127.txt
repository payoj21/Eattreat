3127	Spice It Up With This Dried Garlic & Chilli Chutney	<p>Add a little spice to any meal with this powder chutney!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Servings:</strong> 3-4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">8 cloves of garlic</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup grated dry coconut</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon sesame seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon roasted peanuts</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon coriander powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon tamarind paste</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 teaspoon red chilli powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Heat oil in a pan. Throw in the cloves of garlic and roast for about a minute. Remove and set aside.</p><p>Dry roast the grated dry coconut in the same pan until it turns light brown in colour. Remove and set aside as well.</p><p>Now dry roast the sesame seeds until they begin to pop. This should take no longer than 30 seconds. Remove and set aside.</p><p>Now add the roasted garlic, dry coconut and sesame seeds in a blender along with the roasted peanuts, coriander powder, tamarind paste, red chili powder, and salt. Grind until a medium coarse powder is obtained, and add more salt if required.</p><p>Your chutney is ready! Remember to store this in an airtight container.</p><p><strong>#EatExtra:</strong> Add a little sesame oil and eat with steaming hot idlis, or mix with rice.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
