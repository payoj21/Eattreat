3118	Caramelising Onions Like A Boss In Your Kitchen	<div class="gmail_default"><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Caramelised onions add mouth-watering flavour to almost any dish. From quiches to pizzas, to burgers and grilled cheese sandwiches, this is one ingredient that will take your dish up by a notch. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The process is time consuming, but it’s definitely going to make your cooking game strong.</span></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Onions, butter/ olive oil, vinegar, sugar</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Handpick your onions. Cut off their stems and roots, and slice them into thin slices. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Add three to four tablespoons of fat to a large pan over medium heat. Butter and olive oil both work well, you can use both together as well. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sprinkle some salt for seasoning the onions. This will take out some of the moisture from the onions. Toss the onions so that it’s cooked well. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If the onions stick to the bottom of the pan, deglaze with a tablespoon of red wine vinegar, balsamic vinegar, or wine. Not only will deglazing pull up tasty sticky bits, it will add even more flavour to your caramelised onions.</span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Add a teaspoon of sugar and stir it until it the onions have reached the optimal golden-brown colour. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Store in the fridge, in a sterilised jar or plastic container, and use in your dishes when required, for the perfect taste. </span></p><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">They can be served hot or cold and shelf life is 3 months!</span></p></div>
