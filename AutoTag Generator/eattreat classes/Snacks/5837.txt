5837	Old Delhi’s Chandni Chowk Gets A Makeover With Vehicle-Free Zones & Food Plazas	<p>The Shahjahanabad Redevelopment Corporation (SRDC) recently agreed to beautify a portion of Netaji Subhash Marg, Old Delhi having taken a cue from Amritsar’s heritage corridor project.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>First Things First</strong></h4><p>The SRDC is planning to renovate and beautify a portion of the Netaji Subhash Marg, from the Daryaganj signal to the Lothian Bridge in Old Delhi.</p><p>On both sides of this, they&#8217;ve planned pavements of up to 1 kilometre each in front of Red Fort, which will be spruced up with panels showcasing the history of Shahjahanabad. This area will also have a food plaza, walkways, street furniture, a space for vendors, and dedicated lanes for non-motorised vehicles like cycles and cycle rickshaws.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>On Vehicle Free Zones</strong></h4><p>At a later stage, the authorities plan to convert it into a vehicle-free zone, but for now, only non-destined vehicles won’t be allowed in this stretch. The SRDC officials have also held consultations with the Delhi Traffic Police and have drawn an integrated traffic management plan for compulsory diversions.</p><p>No heavy vehicles or buses will be permitted beyond Delhi Gate or Lothian Bridge Traffic Signal. At the moment, only private vehicles will be allowed to use this road. At a later stage, even private vehicles will be banned from entering. There may even be congestion charges levied so as to deter motorists.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>On Traffic Diversions</strong></h4><p>One can, however, use the Shyama Prasad Mukherjee Marg if you’re coming from the Hanuman Mandir Crossing, and you shall be diverted towards the Rajghat Ring Road if you’re coming from Mathura Road and heading to the Old Delhi Railway Station, the Fatehpuri Masjid, or Kashmere Gate. Other plans for traffic diversions have also been made for the roads connecting to this stretch.</p><p>Read the full story <a href="http://www.hindustantimes.com/delhi/coming-food-plaza-walkways-and-more-at-old-delhi-s-netaji-subhash-marg/story-OmRJ8z9dg19XYb0tiVpHjP.html" target="_blank">here</a>.</p>
