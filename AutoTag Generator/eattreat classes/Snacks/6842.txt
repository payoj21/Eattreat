6842	4 Ways To Eat Extra Creamy Mashed Potatoes When You Have Some Left Over	<p>It&#8217;s a constant struggle to make leftover food interesting, especially in the middle of a week when you&#8217;d rather just get takeaway dinner on your way home from work or order in. Home cooked food needn&#8217;t be a luxury only for the weekends. You can rescue your leftover food from staying inside the fridge forever or from being thrown out with our simple tricks. What better than some mashed potato recipes to start with.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Mashed Potato Waffles</strong></h4><p>Take about two cups of mashed potatoes in a bowl. Combine this with ⅓ cup of refined flour, 1 large egg (lightly beaten), 4 slices of bacon, cooked and chopped, and 3 tablespoons of cheese. Mix them all nicely till you get a sticky dough. Put a small scoop in the middle of your waffle maker, and cook till crispy on the outside, which should take not more than 3-4 minutes. You can have these mashed potato recipes with some half fried eggs, and more bacon (because, why not?).</p><hr /><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Fried Mashed Potato Balls</strong></h4><p><a href="http://eattreat.in/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/Bitterballen_mosterd_mayo-1.jpg"><img title = "4 Ways To Eat Extra Creamy Mashed Potatoes When You Have Some Left Over  "class="alignnone wp-image-6851 size-full" src="http://eattreat.in/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/Bitterballen_mosterd_mayo-1.jpg" alt = "savoury pancakes fried Category:Snacks breakfast baked   " width="847" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/Bitterballen_mosterd_mayo-1.jpg 847w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/Bitterballen_mosterd_mayo-1-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/Bitterballen_mosterd_mayo-1-768x460.jpg 768w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/Bitterballen_mosterd_mayo-1-825x493.jpg 825w" sizes="(max-width: 847px) 100vw, 847px" /></a></p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Wikimedia Commons</strong></em></p><p>Scoop mashed potato and roll them into tiny balls. Beat an egg and dip the balls into them, one by one. Next, roll them in lightly seasoned refined flour and dip them in the beaten egg again. Finally, coat the balls with breadcrumbs and fry.</p><hr /><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Mashed Potato Pancakes</strong></h4><p>Beat two eggs and mix them with the your respective mashed potato recipes. Add ¼ cup flour and seasoning. You can load it up with fillers like bacon, scallions, etc. at this point. Mix well and use the batter to make crispy golden pancakes.</p><hr /><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Mashed Potato Breakfast Muffins</strong></h4><p>In a lightly greased muffin tray, put scoops of mashed potatoes and load them with bacon, mushrooms, cheddar cheese, and anything else that you like. Bake for 15 minutes at 375F (180-190C), or until you can see crispy golden tops.</p><p><em>Featured Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Stephanie via Flickr</strong></em></p>
