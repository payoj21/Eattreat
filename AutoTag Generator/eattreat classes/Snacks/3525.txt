3525	An Easy Paneer Bhurji Recipe For Those Tiffin-Time Woes	<p>We have a very simple recipe for delicious scrambled paneer for you to enjoy on days when you don&#8217;t feel like making too much of an effort to have a scrumptious meal.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 3</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 15 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon cumin seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon ginger, grated</p><p style="text-align: center;">4-5 garlic cloves, crushed</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 green chilli, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 medium onions, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 large tomato, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 medium bell pepper, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon garam masala</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon red chilli powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon coriander powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt, to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons milk</p><p style="text-align: center;">250 grams paneer (cottage cheese), crumbled or grated</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon lemon juice</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Heat oil in a pan and add the cumin seeds.</p><p>Once they begin to splutter add the grated ginger, crushed garlic and chopped green chilli, sauté on medium heat for about a minute.</p><p>Add the chopped onion and continue to sauté till they soften.</p><p>Now stir in the chopped tomato and bell pepper, cook for about 2 minutes, once they have softened add the garam masala, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder and salt to taste. Mix well.</p><p>Pour in the milk, after allowing everything to cook for a minute.</p><p>Add the paneer and lemon juice, mix and cook for about 3-4 minutes. Don’t forget to keep stirring; this is done in order to prevent the paneer from sticking to the pan.</p><p>Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with warm naan or roti or even plain!</p><p><strong>#EatExtra:</strong> You can put this in a roti (Indian bread) and roll it up for mess-free eating, or you can even have this with toasted bread.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
