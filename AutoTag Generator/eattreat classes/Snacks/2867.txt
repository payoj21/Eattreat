2867	Conventional Mint, Coriander & Green Mango Chutney With A Tangy Twist	<p>The classic mint and coriander chutney has for ages been the favoured condiment for savoury Indian snacks like the samosas and kachoris. Here&#8217;s how you can make your green chutney taste different, with just a few simple ingredients!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 3-4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 5-10 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">3 cups fresh coriander leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup mint leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">2-3 green chillies, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">6-8 cloves of garlic</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 raw mango, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt, to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon asafoetida (<em>hing)</em></p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon cumin seeds</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Throw in all the ingredients into a blender and grind until a smooth fine paste is obtained! Your chutney is ready – yes it is just that easy! Don’t forget to store the chutney in an airtight container.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p><p>By: Dr. Neha Mathur, <a href="http://www.whiskaffair.com" target="_blank">food blogger </a></p>
