itemprop="recipeInstructions"><li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">After having thoroughly washed the spinach clean, boil in salted water for about 2 minutes. Using a sieve drain out the water and transfer the spinach to a bowl full of cold water and let it rest for about a minute. Using a sieve again drain out the excess water and place the spinach in a blender. Add to this the ginger, green chilies and ¼ cup water.</li><li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">Grind these ingredients until you obtain a smooth green paste, set this spinach puree aside.</li><li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">Heat oil in a pan and add the paneer cubes, shallow fry them till each side turns a light golden brown. Transfer these to a plate and set aside.</li><li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">Add a little more oil to the pan and place the chopped onion, once they turn translucent add the crushed garlic and stir-fry for less than a minute.</li><li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="4">Now add the spinach puree that you had set aside along with some salt and cook for about 2-3 minutes while stirring. Pour in about 1/3 cup water, mix well and bring it to a boil.</li><li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="5">Once the gravy begins to boil add the paneer cubes, after letting this cook for about 3-4 minutes add the lemon juice, fenugreek leaves and mix well.
