5339	Don’t Want To Get Molasses For Gingerbread Cookies? Try Our Recipe!	<p>The best part about winter has to be the copious amounts of food cooked at home, the warm and inviting fragrance of spices and vanilla emanating from kitchen windows, and the gaiety in the air. We feel there’s nothing better than a plateful of gingerbread cookies with your choice of winter drink for when you have your guests over.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups refined flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 teaspoon ginger powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon cinnamon powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon nutmeg powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon baking soda</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">1½ cups butter (room temperature)</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup brown sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup honey</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 egg</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon vanilla extract</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup white sugar (for sprinkling)</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula</strong></h4><p>Mix flour, ginger powder, cinnamon powder, nutmeg powder, baking soda, and salt in a bowl, and set aside.</p><p>In a larger bowl, mix butter and brown sugar until they’re properly mixed and you can’t feel the granular texture of the sugar. Mix in honey, egg, and vanilla extract.</p><p>Add the mixture of dry ingredients that you’d prepared before to the liquid mixture and combine well.</p><p>Shape the dough into a rectangle and wrap with cling film. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours. You can refrigerate it overnight, or up to a week before you use the dough.</p><p>Preheat oven to 180°C, and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.</p><p>Roll out the dough to about ⅛-inch thick, or about the thickness of a Rs. 10 coin.</p><p>Using a cookie cutter, cut out the shapes that you want your cookies to be in. put them on the baking sheet, and bake for about 12 minutes.</p><p>Once done, remove the cookies from the oven. Sprinkle sugar on top, or decorate with icing.</p><p><strong>#EatExtra:</strong> When you take the cookies out of the oven, they will seem soft, but they’ll soon be exactly like regular gingerbread cookies once they’ve cooled.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <b>Pixabay</b></em></p>
