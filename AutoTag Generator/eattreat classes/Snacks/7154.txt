7154	The Secret Superhero Aloo Tikki Needs No Introduction	<p>Indian street favourite &#8211; Deep fried potato patties. Aloo Tikki was, has and will remain one of our favourite Indian&nbsp;street food favourites. Think chaat&#8211;amazing crunchy on the outside and soft on the inside aloo&nbsp;tikkis with a drizzle of red and green chutney. Has this got you craving for some? Try our easy Aloo Tikki recipe!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 6-8</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 15 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">500 gms potato, boiled, peeled and roughly chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon corn flour</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon coriander powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon carom seed powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">350 ml cooking oil</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><b>Formula</b></h4><p>In a bowl, add all the ingredients and mash together. Prepare equal sized patties from this mixture and set aside.</p><p>In a deep frying pan, heat the oil to at least 140 degrees Celsius.</p><p>Slowly and carefully slide the patties into the oil and fry until golden brown on each side.</p><p>Place a paper towel on a plate and rest the fried patties on it to remove the excess oil.</p><p>Cool for a couple of minutes before serving.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of:<strong> Azra Sadr</strong></em></p>
