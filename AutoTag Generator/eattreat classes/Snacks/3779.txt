3779	Social Offline’s Anda Shami Is Egg-xactly How We Like Our Breakfast	<p>A classic mutton shami wrapped in omelette all the way from Social Offline’s breakfast menu!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 15 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 30 mins</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">50 gms channa dal</p><p style="text-align: center;">50 gms mutton keema</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 whole Kashmiri chilli</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 onion, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon garam masala</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon coriander seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">100 ml refined oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ nutmeg</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 jalapeno, sliced into two</p><p style="text-align: center;">20 gms mozzarella</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 egg, whisked</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;">Freshly ground black pepper to taste</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Soak the chana dal overnight in water a day prior.</p><p>Heat oil in a medium saucepan. Add the onion, tomato, and keema, and sauté for around five-seven minutes until the keema is cooked and the mix is browned.</p><p>Now gradually add the chana dal and along with the dry spices (Kashmiri chilli, garam masala, coriander seeds and nutmeg). Cook this mix for another two-three minutes till the spices have blended well. Keep aside for cooling.</p><p>Once cooled, mince the prepared mix in a blender. Make balls of the desired size from the keema mince, create a thumbprint impression, add in some mozzarella, jalapeno and chopped onions- close the ball. Now dip the prepared ball into the whisked egg to coat and bind, and finally deep fry.</p><p>Finally, wrap the freshly prepared fried shami kebab with omelette and stuff into a pav with green chutney!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of:<strong> Azra Sadr</strong></em></p>
