3716	This Imli Chutney Is The Soulmate To Your Samosas	<p>We bring to you, a sweet and sour tamarind chutney to go with just about anything! But of course, we prefer it with our kachoris and samosas and all the pakoras in the world.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 3 &#8211; 4</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 5 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 16 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup tightly packed seedless tamarind</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup seedless dates</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup powdered/ grated jaggery</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cups water</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon roasted cumin powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon coriander powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon dry ginger powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon red chilli powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">Black or rock salt as needed</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Mix the tamarind, dates, and water together, and heat in a pan for about ten minutes on low.</p><p>Add the jaggery and continue to cook until it completely dissolves.</p><p>Now add the roasted cumin, coriander, dry ginger and red chilli powder. Season with salt, stir and simmer for about a minute or two. Remove and set aside to cool.</p><p>Place this mixture in a grinder and blend until you get a fine paste. You can add water if needed.</p><p>Strain the chutney mixture through a strainer for a perfectly smooth consistency, and you’re done. Store in the refrigerator in an airtight container.</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Kunal Chandra</strong></em></p>
