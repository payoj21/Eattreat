7433	Mad About Chhole Bhature? These 13 Delicious Joints Will Have You Covered	<p>We&#8217;re quite divided about whether Chole Bhature is a snack, or a full-fledged meal. Whatever it may be, it sure is a staple for us. We love to get hot plates of these from the corner shops across the road, and <em>thela wallahs</em> that pepper every street. Sometimes, we even go long distances just to get a plate of this. We thought we&#8217;d make it a little easier for you with a whole list of places where you can get Chole Bhature in New Delhi. Tell us if you see your favourites here?&nbsp;</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>1. Imly</strong></h4><p>The quirky decor and the inventive ways of serving street food are what makes Imly unique and popular. Head over for a mouth-watering plate of Chole Bhature and other street food from all over the country.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong> Shop F-20, Hog Market, Rajendra Place, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Say hello at</strong> 011 33105774</p><p><strong>Pay</strong> INR 110 for a plate (approx.)</p><p>Explore <a href="https://www.facebook.com/imlyxpress/" target="_blank">Imly</a>.&nbsp;</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>2. The Masala Trail</strong></h4><p>They&#8217;ve uplifted the basic street food and turned it into a classy dish to have. This is where you can get your favourite plate of Chole Bhature in the confines of an <a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/masala-trail-street-food-in-delhi/" target="_blank">ambience that feels like luxury</a>. The best part is that the rates are quite pocket-friendly.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong> Shop 52, Janpath, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Say hello at</strong> 011 33106128</p><p><strong>Pay</strong> INR 150 for a plate (approx.)</p><p>Explore <a href="https://www.facebook.com/themasalatrail/?fref=ts" target="_blank">The Masala Trail</a></p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>3. Sitaram Diwan Chand</strong></h4><p>Anyone will tell you that Sitaram Diwan Chand is the place to go to for a piping hot plate of Chole Bhature in New Delhi. If you haven&#8217;t already been there, you absolutely must. Loyalists rave about their Chole Bhature and even head over for the <a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/breakfast-menus-restaurants-budget-cheap/" target="_blank">cheap breakfast option</a> it provides.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong> 2246, Chuna Mandi, Paharganj, New Delhi (Also available at Pitampura)</p><p><strong>Say hello at</strong> 011 23587380, 011 23586128</p><p><strong>Pay</strong> INR 70 for a plate (approx.)</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>4. Shyam Sweets</strong></h4><p>Whether it&#8217;s the middle of a hectic week, or a day when most of the shops in Chawri Bazaar are shut, people are always seen here gorging on their delicious Bhature and sipping on the <a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/famous-food-joints-near-chandni-chowk-old-delhi-food-walk/" target="_blank">Lassi</a>. The Bhature are quite big, so you just might want to share it with a friend because it does tend to get a tad too heavy.</p><p><strong>Head to</strong> 112, Barshahbulla Chowk, Near Metro Station, Chawri Bazar, New Delhi</p><p><strong>Say hello at</strong> 011 23268087</p><p><strong>Pay</strong> INR 100 for a plate (approx.)</p><p>Explore<a href="https://www.facebook.com/shyamsweets112/?fref=ts" target="_blank"> Shyam Sweets</a>.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>5. Giani’s Di Hatti</strong></h4><p>An iconic place in Chandni Chowk, it’s impossible for you to have been around this place and not have noticed the people crowding around the counter at all times of the day.
