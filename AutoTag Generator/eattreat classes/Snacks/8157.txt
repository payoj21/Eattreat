8157	Try A Chocolate Vada Pav At This Vada Pav Festival In Delhi!	<p>We love Vada Pav as much as anyone else, and we can&#8217;t help but be excited about this. The Masala Trail by Osama Jalali is not just getting the iconic vada pav from Mumbai. You will be able to see 6 different vada pavs in Delhi, for the very first time!</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>First Things First</strong></h4><p>The Masala Trail was founded with the aim to bring the traditional street food of India under one roof. And so, they’re bringing the Vada Pav Festival for 10 days with the popular snack of Mumbai, right here in the capital.</p><p>The menu, however, does not limit itself only to flavours of Mumbai. While you will see the famous <strong>Chowpatty Se Vada Pav</strong>, you will also see the <strong>Rajasthani Bullet</strong>, <strong>Bengali Babu</strong>, <strong>Punjabi Tadka</strong>, <strong>Chennai Express</strong>,&nbsp;and <strong>Chocolate Pataka</strong>—flavours that Delhiites have never seen in accordance with the humble vada pav.</p><p>The owner and celebrated food critic, Osama Jalali, is sure that the festival will be well received and that the guests will love every single flavour on the menu.</p><p><a href="http://eattreat.in/magazine/masala-trail-street-food-in-delhi/" target="_blank">The Masala Trail is inspired by regional flavours</a> of Hindustan and attempts to bring all the authentic flavours from all parts of the country to one destination, in a hygienic environment.</p><p>The best part about the festival has to be the fact that the prices of the vada pavs start only at INR 20 (for the vada pav from Chowpatty), and go up to INR 60 (for the chocolate vada pav)!</p><p><strong>Head to</strong> MySquare, SelectCity Walk, Saket.
