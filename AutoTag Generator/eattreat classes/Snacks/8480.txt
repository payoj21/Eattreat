8480	Muhammara Is A Syrian Dip Recipe That’ll Go Superbly With Your Grilled Kebabs	<p>An easy dip recipe for your next party! An arabic dip that&#8217;ll add the right heat to your kebabs and tandoori snacks. We are loving this Muhammara dip recipe and hopefully, you will too.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Serves:</strong> 3-4</p><p><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes, overnight resting time</p><h4><strong>Ingredients:</strong></h4><p>1 cup breadcrumbs</p><p>½ cup extra virgin olive oil</p><p>2 teaspoons tahini</p><p>½ cup walnuts</p><p>2 small red bell peppers, chopped</p><p>1 small onion, chopped</p><p>1 tablespoon chili paste</p><p>1½ tablespoons tomato paste</p><p>2½ tablespoons pomegranate molasses</p><p>1½ teaspoons ground cumin</p><p>1½ teaspoons salt&nbsp;</p><h4><strong>Method:</strong></h4><p>Add the breadcrumbs in a bowl and pour in the olive oil and tahini. Mix well, cover and set aside overnight at room temperature.</p><p>Place the walnuts on a lined baking tray and bake in the oven at 200 degrees Celsius for five minutes till crisp. Grind the roasted walnuts in a blender until they reach a texture similar to breadcrumbs, and add to the breadcrumb mixture. Mix together well.</p><p>Place the chopped peppers, onion, chilli paste and tomato paste in a blender and puree. Add this mixture along with the pomegranate molasses, cumin and salt to the breadcrumbs. Give everything a good mix. Drizzle some olive oil over the top and serve with toasted pita bread.&nbsp;</p><p>Chef’s tip: In typical Syrian style, serve your spicy muhammara dip with a platter of grilled kebabs!</p>
