2275	Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees	<p>Mocktail can jazz up any old picnic or Summer day, and we do love to keep hydrated. So here are some of our best finds as well as inventions, that can give you something to look forward to in this heat. Yay or nay?</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>1. Strawberry Mojito</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Fresh strawberries give a refreshing twist to a run-of-the-mill mojito. And we never say no to these fresh flavours and colours of Summer.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-1.jpg"><img title = "Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  "class="alignnone wp-image-2384" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-1-300x175.jpg" alt = "  " width="916" height="534" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-1-300x175.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-1-768x449.jpg 768w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-1.jpg 868w" sizes="(max-width: 916px) 100vw, 916px" /></a></p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 1</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">4-5 strawberries</p><p style="text-align: center;">Ice cubes, to fill a cocktail shaker and tumbler</p><p style="text-align: center;">50ml honey</p><p style="text-align: center;">10-12 fresh mint leaves</p><p style="text-align: center;">20ml freshly squeezed lime juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">30ml soda water</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 slice of lemon for garnish</p><p style="text-align: center;">Blender</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Blend the strawberries in a blender for 30 to 45 seconds, until they turn into a puree. Set aside.</p><p>Fill up a cocktail shaker with ice cubes; add the honey and 10 mint leaves; close the lid and shake vigorously for a minute. Add the strawberry puree and lime juice and shake again for a minute.</p><p>Strain the liquid from the shaker into a short tumbler filled with ice. Add a dash of soda water, garnish with mint leaves, a slice of lime and serve.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>2. Kiwi Breezie</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">The yummy green of a kiwi can be entirely refreshing in itself. But we like to go a step ahead and present the Kiwi Breezie—a delicious and revitalising drink with coconut water.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5.jpg"><img title = "Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  "class="alignnone wp-image-2380" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5-300x180.jpg" alt = "    " width="972" height="583" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5-768x460.jpg 768w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5.jpg 847w" sizes="(max-width: 972px) 100vw, 972px" /></a></p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">2 kiwis, peeled and chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup coconut water</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ tablespoon basil, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 cup ice cubes</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoons sugar (optional)</p><p style="text-align: center;">2-3 fresh basil leaves for garnish</p><p style="text-align: center;">Blender</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Place the chopped kiwi, coconut water, chopped basil, ice cubes and sugar (if using) into a blender, close the lid, and churn at high speed for about a minute or two, until smooth.</p><p>Pour into serving glasses of your choice, garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve!</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>3. Blueberry Lemonade</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">A zesty lemonade made with fresh berries is a drink right out of an Enid Blyton story. But we can assure you, no Faraway Tree goody will taste as wonderful.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 1</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 2 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 20 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">For syrup</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/2 cup water</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup sugar</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup fresh blueberries</p><p style="text-align: center;">For lemonade</p><p style="text-align: center;">Tall glass filled with ice</p><p style="text-align: center;">250ml soda water</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon lime juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">5-6 blueberries for garnish (optional)</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Heat the water and sugar in a pan until the sugar dissolves. Add the blueberries, bring the mixture to a boil and then let simmer on low heat for 10 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool for 10 minutes.</p><p>In a glass filled with ice, pour two tablespoons of the blueberry syrup, lime juice and top it up with soda water. Serve chilled.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>4. Orange Litchi Ginger Fizz</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Well, we believe that there should be no good reason to mess with a standard Ginger Fizz (Big Chill, anyone?). We also believe that this recipe is more than a goo reason. Three distinct flavours combine together to form this marvellous mocktail.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-2.jpg"><img title = "Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  "class="alignnone wp-image-2379" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-2-300x180.jpg" alt = "      " width="845" height="507" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-2-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-2-768x460.jpg 768w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-2.jpg 847w" sizes="(max-width: 845px) 100vw, 845px" /></a></p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 2 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">1/3 cup litchi juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ cup orange juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup ginger water (water boiled with 5gm ginger and cooled)</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/3 cup soda water</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup ice cubes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Add the litchi juice, orange juice, ginger water, soda water and ice cubes into a cocktail shaker and shake vigorously for a minute. Pour into glasses and serve for a very merry summer soiree.</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>5. Apple Water With Thyme</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Water delicately infused with thyme and apple makes for a super refreshing drink. For those looking to avoid sugar and get healthier, this is a pretty great option.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5-1.jpg"><img title = "Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  Mocktails And Coolers With A Twist To Serve At Summer Soirees  "class="alignnone wp-image-2381" src="http://www.eattreat.in/magazine/magazine//wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5-1-300x180.jpg" alt = "        " width="867" height="520" srcset="http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5-1-300x180.jpg 300w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5-1-768x460.jpg 768w, http://eattreat.in/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/mocktails-5-1.jpg 847w" sizes="(max-width: 867px) 100vw, 867px" /></a></p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 1</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 1 min</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 5-7 mins</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>The Elements:</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup water</p><p style="text-align: center;">6-7 thyme sprigs</p><p style="text-align: center;"><b>½ </b>cup apple juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">1/3 cup soda water</p><p style="text-align: center;">7-8 ice cubes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></p><p>Boil the water with six thyme sprigs in a pan. Remove from heat and let cool completely.</p><p>Add the thyme water, apple juice, soda water and ice cubes to a cocktail shaker and shake for half a minute or so.</p><p>Pour into a glass, garnish with thyme and serve.</p><p>Jai Solanki</p><p>Photos Courtesy Of: Madishetty Manasa</p>
