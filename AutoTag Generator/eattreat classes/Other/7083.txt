7083	Aloo Gobi For When You Need Your Ghar Ka Khana To Remind You Of Mom	<p>An alu gobi recipe that never gets old! Well, since we are on a <a href="http://eattreat.in/offbeat-gobi-masala-recipe/">boring food spree</a>, that we&#8217;re trying to make interesting, here&#8217;s an alu gobi recipe for you, that will always keep things spiced up.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2-3</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 10 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time: </strong>20 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements: </strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">Oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 big potatoes, peeled and chopped into small pieces</p><p style="text-align: center;">200 grams cauliflower florets</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 medium tomato, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 ½ teaspoon red chili powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 teaspoon coriander powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon garam masala powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon cumin powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon dry mango powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">Vegetable oil</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the potatoes and cauliflower florets. Fry until they turn crispy and have browned in color.</p><p>Drain out the oil from the pan, leaving only a teaspoon. Now add the chopped tomato and salt and mix well. Cover the pan and leave to cook for about 2 minutes on a low flame. Mix in the red chili, turmeric, coriander, garam masala, cumin and dry mango powder and cook for 2 more minutes. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with warm rotis or rice!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p><p>&nbsp;</p>
