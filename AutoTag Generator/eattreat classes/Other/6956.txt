6956	This Spicy Amritsari Chole Is Going To Add A Hell Lot Of Tadka To Your Life	<p>An Amritsari chole recipe that will add that tadka to your life! This spicy Amritsari chole recipe will go wonderfully with kulchas or bhature. The <a href="http://eattreat.in/punjab-grill-expansion-punjabi-food/">Punjabi inside us</a> is craving this for a weekend breakfast, and instead of looking for a shop, you can quickly make this at home!</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time: </strong>15 minutes, overnight for soaking chick peas</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time:</strong> 40 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">½ cup chick peas, soaked overnight</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon tea powder or 1-2 tea bags, tied in a plain muslin cloth</p><p style="text-align: center;">Salt to taste</p><p style="text-align: center;">Water</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tomatoes, chopped and grinded into a puree</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 small bay leaf</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 black cardamom</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon coriander seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon cumin seeds</p><p style="text-align: center;">4-5 black peppercorns</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 dry red chili</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 clove</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 inch piece cinnamon</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 tablespoon oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 large onion, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 ½ teaspoon ginger-garlic paste</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 green chili, finely chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 ripe tomatoes, chopped and made into puree</p><p style="text-align: center;">¼ teaspoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">½ teaspoon red chili powder</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>Place the soaked chick peas and tied cloth of tea bags in a pressure cooker with water and a little bit of salt. Turn off the heat after 4-5 whistles, remove the tied cloth and drain the excess water and place the chickpeas in a bowl, set aside.</p><p>Mash about 2 tablespoons of chickpeas and set aside as well.</p><p>In a pan, dry roast the bay leaf, cardamom, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, black peppercorns, dry red chili, clove and cinnamon for about a minute. Set aside to cool and then place this in a blender and grind till you obtain a smooth dry masala powder. Set aside.</p><p>Heat oil in a pan over medium heat and add the chopped onion, once it begins to turn a light brown add the ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chili. Mix well. Now add in the tomato puree and let it cook for about 4-5 minutes. Proceed to add the turmeric and red chili powder as well as the dry masala powder that had been set to the side. Sauté for about a minute and then incorporate the boiled chickpeas along with the mashed chick peas and a cup of water. Give everything a good mix, the gravy should be a little thick before you turn off the heat. Serve hot with warm naan or rice and garnish with chopped coriander leaves!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of:<strong> Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
