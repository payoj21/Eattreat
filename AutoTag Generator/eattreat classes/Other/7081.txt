7081	Kashmiri Dum Aloo When The Dreary Weather Is Getting You Down	<p>Spicy baby potatoes for the cold winter weekends. Luchi and a dum aloo recipe never sounded this good to us. This Dum Aloo recipe on a Sunday afternoon makes us feel all kinds of warm and fuzzy. And leftovers for Monday breakfast? There&#8217;s nothing like it.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Serves:</strong> 2-3</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Prep Time:</strong> 20 minutes</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cook Time: </strong>20 minutes</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Elements:</strong></h4><p style="text-align: center;">2 small potatoes</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 medium onion, chopped</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 cloves garlic</p><p style="text-align: center;">¾ inch ginger</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 green chilies, seeded</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 tablespoon lemon juice</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 tablespoon vegetable oil</p><p style="text-align: center;">3 cloves</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 bay leaf</p><p style="text-align: center;">2 cardamom pods</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspoon turmeric powder</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 teaspooon salt</p><p style="text-align: center;">1 ¼ inch cinnamon stick</p><h4 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Formula:</strong></h4><p>In a pot of boiling salted water cook the potatoes for about 10 minutes, drain out the water and prick the potatoes all over with a skewer or tooth pick. Set aside.</p><p>In a blender place the onion, garlic, ginger, chilies, and lemon juice and grind until you have a paste. Set this aside.</p><p>Heat oil in a pan and add the cloves, bay leaf and cardamom pods, once they begin to swell add in the turmeric powder and salt. Stir in the paste that had been set aside and let it all simmer for about 10 minutes.</p><p>Pour in 50ml water and add the cinnamon and potatoes, mix well and then tightly cover the pan with a lid for about 10 minutes. Serve hot with warm rotis or rice!</p><p>You can garnish with a teaspoon of garam masala and 2 tablespoons of chopped coriander leaves stirred into about 100ml (6-7 tablespoons) of yoghurt!</p><p><em>Photo Courtesy Of: <strong>Madishetty Manasa</strong></em></p>
