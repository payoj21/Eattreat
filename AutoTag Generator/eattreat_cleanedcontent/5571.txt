 rich flavourful pakistani and mughlai dishes become even more irresistible in winters and who does it better than karims this establishment is not referred to as iconic for no reason bowlful of stews kebabs and special rotis we can gorge on these all night through so here is a list of 5 curries you need to try when ordering from karims first things first established by haji karimuddin in 1931 karims is now regarded as one of the iconic restaurants in delhi what began as a small shop in gali kababian near jama masjid has now expanded into multiple outlets citywide however not everyone agrees that the legacy has been continued failing to maintain consistency there are varied opinions about karims while some still adore their mughlai fare others feel its washed out but everyone agrees it will always be a place with memories we leave it to you to decide whether you love them or do not but do try these curries before you decide 1 nihari we love the traditional nihari at karims jama masjid and it will always hold a special place in our hearts however the other branches of karims expanded throughout the city are not that great for this stew one other branch which gets it right is in sector 14 gurgaon they make nihari only on weekends but it is quite spectacular #eatextra never eat nihari without its garnishes coriander ginger green chilli and lemon served with this mughlai speciality are not just for eye appeal pair it with khameeri roti for a splendid meal 2 chicken stew a quintessentially winter dish this chicken stew is beautifully creamy rich but full of spices cooked without water in onions and curd this simple dish should not be ignored available at almost all branches of karims you should definitely give it a try this season #eatextra pair it with sheermal to bring out the understated sweetness 3 badam pasanda while all the dishes at karims seem to be floating around in oil this one is particularly not for the ones who loathe the oily layer a you visit 4 chicken jahangiri blazing in a bright shade of red this curry has its own unique taste you will either hate it or love it but the chilli and spices in this curry will definitely hit you
