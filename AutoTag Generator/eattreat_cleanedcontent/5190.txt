 who does not love a good spaghetti carbonara its cheesy its got bacon and its got spaghetti whether you are stuffing yourself with spaghetti carbonara on a chilly winter night or dishing it out to impress your friends at a brunch – we are sure you dig it just as much as we do first things first we will take a moment here for the uninitiated spaghetti carbonara is an italian pasta dish from rome made with eggs cheese pecorino romano or parmigianoreggiano bacon and black pepper now for the initiated did you know that this dish dates to sometime around the 20 th century we have got some facts and folklores about the beloved carbonaras origins carbonara originates from the appenine mountains of abruzzo most of the theories about the carbonara are anecdotal some of them point to the appenine mountains of the abruzzo where the dish seems to have been cooked by woodcutters who made charcoal for fuel they would cook the dish using penne rather than spaghetti as it is easier to toss penne with eggs and cheese it was the staple fare of coal miners other more obvious theories talk of the dish being eaten by coal workers the meaning of alla carbonara is coal workers style and that the use of coarsely ground black pepper resembles coal flakes it was learned by the romans during world war ii there is also a theory which says that in the province of ciociaria halfway between rome and benevento pasta was seasoned neapolitan style with eggs lard and pecorino cheese during the german occupation of rome during ww ii many middleclass families dispersed from rome to this region to escape and learned about this dish after the war roman cuisine became very popular throughout italy and the carbonara was a prime example it came from the famous restaurant in rome la carbonara another tale talks of how the famous restaurant in rome la carbonara was named after its speciality though the restaurant has been open in rome since early 20 th century and does have carbonara on its menu the restaurant itself denies any such connection and tells us the name came about for other reasons whatever carbonaras origins may be one thing is for sure – we are glad it exists it is a fairly simple dish to prepare so you need not be cooking up a storm in your kitchen you can serve it with some bruschetta on the side or some roasted asparagus you could serve it by itself too its a winning dish ☺ you can try some in delhi here did you know the spaghetti carbonara has really tumultuous origins
