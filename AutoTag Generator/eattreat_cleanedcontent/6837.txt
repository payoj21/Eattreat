 delhi food walks went on one of their food expeditions around town and this time it was kumar samose wala doing fusion street food with 32 kinds of samosas so what do think happened we gorged of course i would not be wrong in saying that every delhite has had atleast one samosa in their lifetime most of us have had the same spicy mashed potato filling wrapped in deep fried crusty yet soft shell right well i present to you an individual who has changed the way samosas taste the range of samosas he offers has customers lining up outside his shop every single day the concept is so simple yet intriguing you ca not help but have a bite whats so new you must have had pizza pasta and chowmein as standalone dishes but have you ever had them inside a samosa the answer is no is not it these are the variants of samosas they serve apart from using malai chaap malai paneer paneer korma and even chocolate as fillings yes you read that right now that your mouth is sufficiently salivated i shall proceed to tell you how they taste so you can slurp as you read on the first one i tried was the malai chaap samosa its outward appearance was similar to the usual samosa upon crushing it from the middle small bits of chaap covered in a paste of malai were visible even though the thought of such a dish was fascinating i still had my doubts about the taste but it was definitely the best samosa and the best malai chaap i have ever had the salty chaap covered in slightly marinated malai cream was a warm welcome to the taste buds and the crispy samosa shell which held this amazing filling went along really well in terms of taste i also tried the chowmein samosa which is basically noodles fried in hot spices and veggies and then wrapped in a samosa the overall experience was like having bitesized chowmein parcels which was awesome owner speak i asked the owner mr deep bhutani ji how he came up with the idea of making this fusion street food to which he explained that people had started to gravitate towards other snacks– foreign and native alike as samosas have had the same taste since their inception so he decided to change the way samosas tasted and decided to bring variety to this historic dish the price ranges from inr 15 to 20 which is super affordable considering the novelty of idea and quality of the samosas thats the reason why the shop is perpetually thronged by customers between 530 pm to 900 pm the name of the shop is ‘kumar samose and it is near the moti nagar metro station anybody would be happy to guide you to this fusion street food head to
