 now get all the regional flavours of india and colourful handicraft products under one roof at hunar haatan initiative by the ministry of minority affairs to encourage and promote local artisans and cuisines on a nationwide and international level at this food festival in delhi first things first the union minister of the ministry of minority affairs mukhtar abbas naqvi inaugurated the exhibition in the capital on saturday the 11th of february this is the second edition of hunar haat and the first one happened in november 2016 in pragati maidan the minister is confident that this time too the festival will be well received the festival showcases handicrafts and handloom products from 24 states and union territories and has about 130 stalls for the very first time at this festival you are going to see exquisite works like the makrana marble products bandhej from sikar mojri from rajasthan banjara embroidery from telangana handmade locks and door handles and much more these master artisans belong to uttar pradesh odisha assam kerala telangana bihar etc you can browse through these handicraft and handloom products while munching on the delectable treats from the food stalls that are put up around the exhibition the bawarchikhana is showcasing food from 13 states and you can find old favourites and never seen before food here what to eat you can get awadhi mughlai foods from lucknow dal bati churma and thali from rajasthan sandesh and rasogolla from west bengal malabari food from kerala litti chokha of bihar puran poli from maharashtra dhokla and jalebi from gujarat kashmiri wazwan from jammu and kashmir bhutte ke khees and sabudana kheer and khichri from madhya pradesh at the exhibition head to state emporia complex baba kharak singh marg connaught place dates 11th 26th feb explore hunar haat representative photo courtesy of wikimedia commons hunar haat street food and regional fare from 13 states at this brand new festival
